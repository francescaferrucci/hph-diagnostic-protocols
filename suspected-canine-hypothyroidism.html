Diagnostic Algorithm

Suspected Canine Hypothyroidism

Protocol-aligned workflow for dogs with clinical signs suggestive of hypothyroidism.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Suspected canine hypothyroidism is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically run Total T4, Free T4 by equilibrium dialysis, cTSH, thyroid autoantibodies, CBC, chemistry profile, electrolytes, urinalysis, or follow-up thyroid testing unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
SUSPECTED CANINE HYPOTHYROIDISM

History

  • Weight gain or difficulty losing weight
  • Low energy, dullness, or exercise intolerance
  • Hair loss, dull coat, recurrent skin/ear issues
  • Cold intolerance or behavior change
  • Current medications or recent illness
  • Prior thyroid testing or supplementation history

Physical Exam

  • Body condition and weight trend
  • Skin and coat quality
  • Heart rate and vital parameters
  • Evidence of concurrent illness
  • Ear/skin infection signs
  • Neurologic or orthopedic concerns if present

Protocol Check

  • Is the Pet not eating and meeting age/duration criteria?
  • Respiratory distress?
  • Pale mucous membranes?
  • Bleeding, toxin, or pyometra concern?
  • Vomiting or diarrhea trigger?
  • CMO approval needed for thyroid testing?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does Another Hannah Protocol Trigger Apply?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

CBC + SHORT PROFILE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and less than 1 year old
  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and over 8 years old
  • ☐ Not eating for 72 hrs and 2–8 years old
  • ☐ Productive vomiting for more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Diarrhea trigger applies

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not run automatic thyroid or baseline diagnostics.
  • ☐ Document clinical signs and exam findings.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
↓
STEP 2

If Thyroid Testing Is Requested

Total T4, Free T4 by equilibrium dialysis, cTSH, thyroid autoantibodies, follow-up T4 testing, and thyroid medication monitoring are above the Diagnostic Testing Protocols unless specifically approved. Submit the request and wait for CMO approval before proceeding.

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Total T4
  • ☐ Free T4 by equilibrium dialysis
  • ☐ cTSH
  • ☐ T3/T4 autoantibodies
  • ☐ Repeat or follow-up T4 testing
  • ☐ CBC/chemistry/electrolytes when no protocol trigger applies

DOCUMENTATION

  • ☐ Record clinical signs supporting suspicion.
  • ☐ Note concurrent illness or NTI concern.
  • ☐ Document medications that could affect thyroid results.
  • ☐ Follow CMO-approved plan once received.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Hypothyroid Signs Alone

No automatic thyroid diagnostics are approved for hypothyroid signs alone. Document clinical signs and submit Diagnostic & Requisition request if testing is needed.

Concurrent Not-Eating Trigger

If the Pet meets the age and duration criteria for not eating, CBC and Short Profile are protocol-approved. Thyroid testing still requires CMO approval.

Concurrent Vomiting or Diarrhea Trigger

Follow the vomiting or diarrhea workflow when those triggers apply. Run only the specific protocol-approved diagnostics for that presentation.

Pale Mucous Membranes or Respiratory Distress

CBC is protocol-approved if pale mucous membranes or respiratory distress are present. X-rays are also protocol-approved for respiratory distress.

Possible Nonthyroidal Illness

NTI can lower T4. Document concurrent illness and route diagnostics through the appropriate workflow or Diagnostic & Requisition request.

Follow-Up Thyroid Monitoring

Follow-up T4 testing, dose adjustment support, or additional thyroid panels should follow the CMO-approved monitoring plan.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Total T4, Free T4 by equilibrium dialysis, cTSH, T3/T4 autoantibodies, repeat or follow-up T4, CBC when no protocol trigger applies, chemistry profile, electrolytes, urinalysis, dermatologic workup, endocrine panels, referral diagnostics, thyroid medication monitoring, or other diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Monitoring / Escalation

Reassess if clinical signs worsen, concurrent illness develops, medication changes occur, or the Pet fails to respond to a CMO-approved plan. Document response, client communication, recheck timing, and any approved dose adjustments.

Critter Clues

  • Canine hypothyroidism should be based on clinical signs and appropriate testing.
  • NTI can lower T4 and complicate interpretation.
  • Thyroid testing is not automatic under current Diagnostic Testing Protocols.
  • Follow-up thyroid monitoring should follow a CMO-approved plan.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history and document clinical signs.
  2. Perform physical exam and assess for concurrent illness.
  3. Determine whether another protocol trigger applies.
  4. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  5. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for thyroid diagnostics above protocol.
  6. Route for CMO approval when required.
  7. Support sample collection and monitoring only after approval.
  8. Document findings, approvals, client conversations, treatment response, and rechecks.

Protocol Summary

Hypothyroid signs alone: No automatic diagnostics
Total T4 / Free T4 / cTSH: CMO approval required
Approved not-eating/vomiting/diarrhea trigger: CBC + Short Profile
Respiratory distress / pale MM: CBC allowed
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
