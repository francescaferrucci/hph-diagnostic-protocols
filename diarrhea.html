Diagnostic Algorithm

Diarrhea

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets presenting with diarrhea.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Automatic diagnostics for diarrhea are limited by Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. For Dogs or Cats over 1 year old with diarrhea for more than 72 hours, CBC and Short Profile are protocol-approved. For puppies under 1 year old with diarrhea and no vaccines, CBC, Parvo, and Diff are protocol-approved. Any diagnostics beyond these triggers require a Diagnostic & Requisition request and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
DIARRHEA

History

  • Duration of diarrhea
  • Frequency and stool character
  • Blood, mucus, melena, or straining
  • Diet change, treats, garbage, toxins, or medications
  • Vomiting, anorexia, lethargy, fever, or weight loss
  • Vaccination status for puppies

Physical Exam

  • Hydration status
  • Vital parameters
  • Abdominal pain or distension
  • Body condition and weight change
  • Rectal exam when appropriate
  • Systemic illness signs

Protocol Check

  • Is the Pet over 1 year old?
  • Has diarrhea lasted more than 72 hours?
  • Is this a puppy under 1 year old?
  • Is the puppy unvaccinated?
  • Are there severe or unstable signs?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does the Pet Meet a Diarrhea Testing Trigger?

DOG/CAT > 1 YEAR + DIARRHEA > 72 HOURS

  • ☐ CBC
  • ☐ Short Profile
  • ☐ Document duration and clinical signs

PUPPY < 1 YEAR + NO VACCINES

  • ☐ CBC
  • ☐ Parvo test
  • ☐ Diff
  • ☐ Document vaccine status

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not add bloodwork, fecal testing, Giardia, imaging, cPLI, T4, or other diagnostics automatically.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before proceeding.
↓
STEP 2

Assess Severity and Escalation Need

If the Pet is unstable, dehydrated, painful, febrile, lethargic, losing weight, has bloody diarrhea, melena, persistent vomiting, or worsening signs, escalate immediately while staying within protocol-approved diagnostics unless CMO approval is obtained.

LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Diarrhea Less Than 72 Hours

No automatic bloodwork or additional diagnostics unless another Hannah protocol trigger applies. Provide supportive care and monitoring as directed.

Dog/Cat Over 1 Year with Diarrhea More Than 72 Hours

Protocol-approved diagnostics: CBC and Short Profile. Document duration, hydration status, physical exam findings, and any systemic signs.

Puppy Under 1 Year with No Vaccines

Protocol-approved diagnostics: CBC, Parvo, and Diff. Escalate promptly due to infectious disease risk and patient vulnerability.

Diarrhea with Vomiting

Follow the Acute Vomiting workflow if vomiting triggers apply. Productive vomiting more than 48 hours allows CBC and Short Profile; x-rays are allowed for vomiting more than 48 hours, non-productive vomiting, or suspected foreign body.

Straining to Defecate

X-rays are protocol-approved for straining to defecate or no defecation for 48 hours. Additional diagnostics still require CMO approval unless another protocol trigger applies.

Severe or Systemic Illness

Stabilize and escalate. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics unless CMO approval is obtained for additional testing.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Fecal exam, fecal cytology, Giardia ELISA, fecal culture, internal organ screen, electrolytes, urinalysis, FeLV/FIV, T4, cPLI, TLI, cobalamin/folate, albumin workup, abdominal radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, abdominal ultrasound, endoscopy, biopsy, exploratory surgery, infectious disease testing, or referral diagnostics require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Reassessment/Escalation

If diarrhea persists, worsens, becomes bloody, is associated with lethargy, fever, dehydration, vomiting, weight loss, abdominal pain, or poor response to initial care, reassess and escalate. Additional diagnostics require CMO approval unless a protocol trigger applies.

Critter Clues

  • Diarrhea is a clinical sign, not a diagnosis.
  • Duration matters: more than 72 hours changes testing eligibility.
  • Puppies without vaccines require special caution.
  • Do not automatically add fecal, Giardia, imaging, or specialty GI diagnostics.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain complete history.
  2. Perform physical exam and rectal exam when appropriate.
  3. Determine duration of diarrhea.
  4. Confirm age and vaccination status.
  5. Screen for dehydration, fever, lethargy, vomiting, blood, melena, or weight loss.
  6. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  7. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatments, and response.

Protocol Summary

Dog/Cat >1 year + diarrhea >72 hrs: CBC + Short Profile
Puppy <1 year + no vaccines: CBC + Parvo + Diff
Straining/no defecation 48 hrs: X-rays allowed
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
