Diagnostic Algorithm

Polyuria / Polydipsia (PU/PD)

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with increased urination and/or increased thirst.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

PU/PD is listed in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Protocol-approved diagnostics are CBC and Profile with Electrolytes. Do not automatically add urinalysis, urine culture, T4, glucose curve, fructosamine, bile acids, ACTH stimulation testing, LDDS, UCCR, imaging, ultrasound, fecal testing, endoscopy, biopsy, or referral diagnostics unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
PU/PD — INCREASED URINATION OR THIRST

History

  • Onset, duration, and severity
  • Measured water intake if possible
  • Urination frequency, volume, accidents, or urgency
  • Appetite, weight change, vomiting, diarrhea, or lethargy
  • Medications, supplements, diet, or toxin exposure
  • Prior kidney, endocrine, urinary, or liver disease

Physical Exam

  • Hydration status
  • Body weight and body condition
  • Temperature and vital parameters
  • Abdominal palpation
  • Signs of systemic illness
  • Neurologic or pain concerns if present

Protocol Check

  • PU/PD confirmed by history?
  • Any urinary straining?
  • Any no urination for 48 hours?
  • Not eating trigger met?
  • Vomiting or diarrhea trigger met?
  • CMO approval needed for diagnostics above protocol?
↓

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does the Pet Have PU/PD?

YES — PROTOCOL-APPROVED

  • ☐ CBC
  • ☐ Profile with Electrolytes
  • ☐ Document water intake if known
  • ☐ Document urination changes and clinical signs

NO CLEAR PU/PD

  • ☐ Do not run automatic PU/PD diagnostics.
  • ☐ Clarify history and clinical concern.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request if testing is needed.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
↓
STEP 2

Are Additional Diagnostics Needed?

PU/PD allows CBC and Profile with Electrolytes. Any additional diagnostic workup to identify the cause requires CMO approval unless another listed protocol trigger applies.

OTHER TRIGGERS MAY APPLY

  • ☐ Straining to urinate: x-rays allowed
  • ☐ No urination for 48 hours: x-rays allowed
  • ☐ Productive vomiting >48 hrs: CBC + Short Profile
  • ☐ Diarrhea >72 hrs: CBC + Short Profile
  • ☐ Not eating trigger: CBC + Short Profile

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Urinalysis or urine culture
  • ☐ T4 or endocrine testing
  • ☐ Glucose curve or fructosamine
  • ☐ Bile acids, ACTH stim, LDDS, or UCCR
  • ☐ Radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers
  • ☐ Ultrasound, biopsy, referral, or advanced diagnostics
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Confirmed PU/PD

Protocol-approved diagnostics: CBC and Profile with Electrolytes. Document onset, water intake, urination changes, medications, diet, and exam findings.

PU/PD + Urinary Straining

X-rays are protocol-approved for straining to urinate. Additional urinary diagnostics, including urinalysis or culture, require CMO approval unless separately authorized.

PU/PD + No Urination for 48 Hours

X-rays are protocol-approved. Escalate promptly due to risk of obstruction or serious urinary disease.

PU/PD + Vomiting, Diarrhea, or Not Eating

Follow the matching workflow if those criteria are met. Run only the specific protocol-approved diagnostics for that presentation.

Possible Diabetes, Kidney, Liver, or Endocrine Disease

Additional testing to confirm or differentiate these causes is above protocol and requires Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Monitoring / Recheck

Recheck bloodwork, urinalysis, water intake, endocrine testing, or additional monitoring should follow the CMO-approved plan.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Urinalysis, urine culture, Total T4, glucose curve, fructosamine, bile acids, ACTH stimulation test, LDDS, UCCR, creatinine clearance, modified water deprivation test, DDAVP trial, abdominal radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, abdominal ultrasound, fecal testing, Giardia, cPL, TLI, cobalamin/folate, endoscopy, biopsy, exploratory surgery, referral diagnostics, or other PU/PD workup require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Reassessment / Escalation

Escalate if PU/PD is worsening, the Pet becomes lethargic, stops eating, vomits, loses weight, strains to urinate, cannot urinate, becomes dehydrated, or develops abnormal vitals. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • PU/PD is a sign, not a diagnosis.
  • Measure water intake whenever possible.
  • CBC and Profile with Electrolytes are protocol-approved for PU/PD.
  • Urinalysis and endocrine testing are not automatic under the protocol.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history and measure water intake if possible.
  2. Perform physical exam and assess hydration/stability.
  3. Confirm whether PU/PD is present.
  4. Run protocol-approved CBC and Profile with Electrolytes.
  5. Check whether another protocol trigger applies.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatments, and follow-up plan.

Protocol Summary

PU/PD: CBC + Profile with Electrolytes
Straining/no urination trigger: X-rays allowed
Urinalysis/culture/T4/endocrine tests: CMO approval required
Imaging outside listed triggers: CMO approval required
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
