Ascites

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with abdominal fluid, swelling, or distension.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Ascites is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically run CBC/diff, internal organ function screen, electrolytes, urinalysis, fecal testing, 3Dx, FeLV/FIV, abdominocentesis, cytology, fluid analysis, abdominal ultrasound, bile acids, cultures, coagulation testing, ACTH stimulation testing, cPL, exploratory surgery, or referral diagnostics unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
ASCITES / ABDOMINAL DISTENSION

History

  • Onset, duration, and progression of abdominal distension
  • Appetite, weight change, vomiting, diarrhea, or lethargy
  • Coughing, exercise intolerance, collapse, or breathing changes
  • Trauma, toxin exposure, or possible bleeding concern
  • Urination, thirst, and defecation changes
  • Known liver, heart, kidney, cancer, or endocrine history

Physical Exam

  • Hydration and perfusion status
  • Mucous membrane color
  • Respiratory effort and thoracic auscultation
  • Cardiovascular exam and pulse quality
  • Abdominal palpation and fluid wave assessment
  • Pain, fever, jaundice, edema, or systemic illness signs

Protocol Check

  • Respiratory distress?
  • Pale mucous membranes?
  • Bleeding, toxin, or pyometra concern?
  • Not eating trigger met?
  • Vomiting, diarrhea, PU/PD, or urinary trigger?
  • Trauma or tumor suspect?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does Another Hannah Protocol Trigger Apply?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

CBC + SHORT PROFILE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and less than 1 year old
  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and over 8 years old
  • ☐ Not eating for 72 hrs and 2–8 years old
  • ☐ Productive vomiting for more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Dog/Cat over 1 year with diarrhea for more than 72 hrs

OTHER APPROVED TRIGGERS

  • ☐ PU/PD: CBC + Profile with Electrolytes
  • ☐ Blocked cat: Profile only
  • ☐ Puppy diarrhea/no vaccines: CBC + Parvo + Diff
  • ☐ Listed x-ray trigger applies

ASCITES ALONE

  • ☐ No automatic diagnostics are approved for ascites alone.
  • ☐ Document history, exam findings, and stability assessment.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
↓
STEP 2

Are X-Rays Protocol-Approved?

X-rays are not automatically approved for ascites alone. Use x-rays only when a listed x-ray trigger applies, such as respiratory distress, tumor suspect, vehicular trauma, vomiting more than 48 hours, non-productive vomiting, suspected foreign body, straining to urinate/defecate, or no urination/defecation for 48 hours.

X-RAYS APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Tumor suspect, 3 views
  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Obvious broken bone or dislocated joint
  • ☐ Vomiting more than 48 hrs / non-productive vomiting / suspected foreign body
  • ☐ Straining or no urination/defecation trigger

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Abdominocentesis
  • ☐ Fluid cytology or fluid analysis
  • ☐ Abdominal ultrasound
  • ☐ Bile acids or urine Pr:Cr
  • ☐ Cultures, coag testing, ACTH, cPL, or exploratory surgery
  • ☐ Any diagnostics above listed protocol triggers
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Ascites Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for ascites alone. Document abdominal distension, respiratory status, cardiovascular findings, mucous membrane color, pain, and overall stability. Submit request if diagnostics are needed.

Ascites + Respiratory Distress

CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved if respiratory distress is present. Escalate promptly because abdominal fluid may compromise breathing or indicate serious systemic disease.

Ascites + Pale Mucous Membranes or Bleeding Concern

CBC is protocol-approved if pale mucous membranes or bleeding concern are present. Coagulation testing, fluid analysis, imaging, or transfusion planning require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Ascites + Not Eating, Vomiting, or Diarrhea Trigger

Follow the matching workflow if the Pet meets those criteria. Run only the specific protocol-approved diagnostics for that presentation.

Ascites + PU/PD

PU/PD is protocol-approved for CBC and Profile with Electrolytes. Additional ascites workup such as UA, urine Pr:Cr, ultrasound, bile acids, or fluid analysis requires CMO approval.

Ascites + Tumor Suspect

Three-view radiographs are protocol-approved if tumor suspect. Cytology, fluid analysis, ultrasound, biopsy, staging, or referral diagnostics require CMO approval.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

CBC/diff when no protocol trigger applies, internal organ function screen, electrolytes, urinalysis, fecal flotation, saline smear, 3Dx, FeLV/FIV, chest or abdominal radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, abdominocentesis, in-house cytology, fluid analysis, abdominal ultrasound, echocardiography, bile acids, urine Pr:Cr, fluid culture, coagulation panel, BMBT, ACT, PT/PTT, T4/Free T4, SNAP cPL, ACTH stimulation testing, serum creatinine vs. abdominal fluid creatinine, biopsy, exploratory surgery, transfusion planning, or referral diagnostics require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Monitoring / Escalation

Escalate immediately if the Pet has respiratory distress, collapse, pale mucous membranes, abdominal pain, active bleeding, shock signs, severe lethargy, inability to urinate, or rapidly worsening abdominal distension. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Ascites can result from increased fluid production or decreased fluid removal.
  • Assess respiratory effort and cardiovascular status early.
  • Fluid classification is useful, but fluid analysis is above protocol.
  • X-rays are only automatic when a listed x-ray trigger applies.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history.
  2. Perform complete physical exam, including respiratory and cardiovascular assessment.
  3. Assess stability, pain, mucous membrane color, and abdominal distension.
  4. Determine whether another protocol trigger applies.
  5. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for ascites diagnostics above protocol.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, approvals, treatments, communication, and follow-up plan.

Protocol Summary

Ascites alone: No automatic diagnostics
Respiratory distress: CBC + x-rays allowed
Pale MM / bleeding / toxin / pyometra: CBC allowed
PU/PD: CBC + Profile with Electrolytes
Abdominocentesis/fluid analysis/US: CMO approval required
