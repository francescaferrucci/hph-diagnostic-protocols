Diagnostic Algorithm

Red Eye

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets presenting with a red, painful, swollen, or abnormal eye.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Red Eye is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically perform tonometry, fluorescein stain, Schirmer tear test, cytology, culture, bloodwork, infectious disease testing, imaging, or referral diagnostics unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
PATIENT WITH RED EYE

History

  • Onset and duration
  • One eye or both eyes
  • Discharge, squinting, rubbing, or pawing
  • Vision change or bumping into objects
  • Trauma, foreign body, or chemical exposure
  • Current medications or prior eye disease

Initial Exam

  • Assess comfort and pain level
  • Look for squinting or light sensitivity
  • Assess discharge and swelling
  • Check for cloudiness or visible injury
  • Assess whether vision appears affected
  • Document appearance of both eyes

Emergency Red Flags

  • Severe pain or squinting
  • Vision loss or sudden blindness
  • Corneal ulcer or perforation concern
  • Hyphema or blood in the eye
  • Proptosis
  • Significant trauma

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Is This an Ocular Emergency?

YES — PROTECT / ESCALATE

  • ☐ Apply e-collar if indicated.
  • ☐ Prevent rubbing or self-trauma.
  • ☐ Notify medical leadership immediately.
  • ☐ Do not delay escalation for diagnostics.
  • ☐ Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

NO — CONTINUE TRIAGE

  • ☐ Complete history and exam documentation.
  • ☐ Determine unilateral vs. bilateral.
  • ☐ Screen for systemic signs.
  • ☐ Check for another protocol trigger.
↓
STEP 2

Does Another Hannah Protocol Trigger Apply?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

X-RAYS APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Significant trauma meets x-ray trigger
  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Obvious broken bone or dislocated joint
  • ☐ Other listed x-ray trigger applies

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not run automatic ocular diagnostics.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Red Eye Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for red eye alone. Document history, exam findings, pain level, discharge, laterality, and whether vision appears affected. Submit request if diagnostics are needed.

Emergency Red Flags Present

Protect the eye, prevent self-trauma, notify leadership immediately, and follow urgent direction. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Red Eye + Trauma

If trauma meets a listed x-ray trigger, radiographs are protocol-approved. Ocular diagnostics such as stain, tonometry, or referral still require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Red Eye + Bleeding Concern

CBC is protocol-approved if there is a bleeding concern. Coagulation testing, ocular diagnostics, imaging, or referral diagnostics require CMO approval.

Red Eye + Systemic Signs

If another protocol trigger applies, follow that workflow. Otherwise, systemic workup, infectious disease testing, and advanced diagnostics require CMO approval.

Not Improving / Diagnosis Uncertain

Reassess, document progression, and submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for further diagnostics or ophthalmology referral consideration.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Tonometry, fluorescein stain, Schirmer tear test, ocular cytology, ocular culture, bloodwork when no protocol trigger applies, infectious disease testing, imaging outside listed x-ray triggers, ophthalmology referral, advanced ocular diagnostics, or treatment trials above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Monitoring / Escalation

Escalate promptly if the Pet develops worsening pain, squinting, cloudiness, vision change, bleeding in the eye, swelling, discharge, proptosis, trauma history, or failure to improve. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Red eye can represent minor irritation or an emergency.
  • Severe pain, vision loss, hyphema, ulcer/perforation concern, proptosis, or trauma should be escalated immediately.
  • Protect the eye from rubbing or self-trauma.
  • Do not automatically perform ophthalmic diagnostics above protocol.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain complete history.
  2. Perform physical exam and basic ocular observation.
  3. Screen for emergency red flags.
  4. Protect the eye and escalate immediately if red flags are present.
  5. Determine whether another protocol trigger applies.
  6. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  7. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  8. Document findings, approvals, treatments, and follow-up plan.

Protocol Summary

Red eye alone: No automatic diagnostics
Bleeding concern: CBC allowed
Trauma with listed x-ray trigger: X-rays allowed
Tonometry/stain/STT/cytology/culture: CMO approval required
Ophthalmology referral diagnostics: CMO approval required
