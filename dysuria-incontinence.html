Diagnostic Algorithm

Dysuria / Incontinence

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with urinary straining, accidents, dribbling, or abnormal urination.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Under Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols, x-rays are protocol-approved for straining to urinate or no urination for 48 hours. Blocked cat allows Profile Only. Dysuria or incontinence alone does not automatically authorize CBC/diff, internal organ screen, urinalysis, urine culture, viral testing, catheterization, contrast cystogram, ultrasound, IVP, urodynamic studies, spinal radiographs, exploratory cystotomy, or other diagnostics. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
DYSURIA / INCONTINENCE

History

  • Straining, frequent attempts, accidents, dribbling, or inappropriate urination
  • Ability to pass urine
  • Time since last normal urination
  • Blood in urine or painful urination
  • Water intake changes or PU/PD
  • Trauma, neurologic signs, medications, or prior urinary disease

Physical Exam

  • Hydration and vital parameters
  • Abdominal palpation and bladder size
  • Pain assessment
  • Mucous membrane color
  • Rectal exam when appropriate
  • Basic neurologic screen if incontinence is reported

Protocol Check

  • Straining to urinate?
  • No urination for 48 hours?
  • Blocked cat?
  • PU/PD present?
  • Pale mucous membranes, toxin, trauma, or systemic illness?
  • CMO approval needed for diagnostics above protocol?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Is This a Urinary Obstruction Concern?

BLOCKED CAT

  • ☐ Profile Only is protocol-approved.
  • ☐ Escalate immediately.
  • ☐ Document urination history and bladder findings.
  • ☐ Additional diagnostics require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

X-RAYS APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Straining to urinate
  • ☐ No urination for 48 hours
  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Other listed x-ray trigger applies

NO OBSTRUCTION / NO X-RAY TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not run automatic urinary diagnostics.
  • ☐ Document history, exam findings, and stability.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request if diagnostics are needed.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
↓
STEP 2

Does Another Protocol Trigger Apply?

PU/PD

  • ☐ CBC
  • ☐ Profile with Electrolytes
  • ☐ Urinalysis still requires CMO approval unless separately authorized.

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Urinalysis or urine culture
  • ☐ CBC/diff or chemistry when no trigger applies
  • ☐ Catheterization for diagnostic purposes
  • ☐ Contrast cystogram, IVP, urodynamic studies
  • ☐ Ultrasound, spinal radiographs, exploratory cystotomy
  • ☐ Any diagnostics above listed protocol triggers
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Blocked Cat

Protocol-approved diagnostic: Profile Only. Escalate immediately due to obstruction risk. Additional diagnostics require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Straining to Urinate

X-rays are protocol-approved. Document frequency of attempts, urine output, pain, bladder palpation findings, and overall stability.

No Urination for 48 Hours

X-rays are protocol-approved. Escalate promptly because inability to urinate may be an emergency.

Incontinence Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for incontinence alone. Document pattern, duration, neurologic concerns, medications, and whether the Pet can urinate normally.

Dysuria/Incontinence + PU/PD

PU/PD is protocol-approved for CBC and Profile with Electrolytes. Urinalysis, culture, imaging, and endocrine testing require CMO approval unless another trigger applies.

Dysuria/Incontinence + Trauma or Neuro Concern

X-rays are protocol-approved only when a listed trigger applies, such as vehicular trauma or paresis/paralysis. Advanced neuro or urinary diagnostics require CMO approval.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Viral testing, CBC/diff when no protocol trigger applies, internal organ screen, urinalysis, urine culture, catheterization for diagnostic purposes, contrast cystogram, IVP, urodynamic studies, abdominal ultrasound, spinal radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, exploratory cystotomy, inappropriate elimination diagnostics, advanced neurologic workup, referral diagnostics, or other urinary diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Monitoring / Escalation

Escalate immediately if the Pet cannot urinate, is repeatedly straining with little/no urine, has a painful or firm bladder, is vomiting, lethargic, weak, collapsed, painful, or has abnormal vitals. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Straining to urinate can indicate an emergency.
  • Blocked cats require immediate escalation.
  • Incontinence can be behavioral, urinary, or neurologic.
  • X-rays are protocol-approved for straining or no urination for 48 hours.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed urinary history.
  2. Perform physical exam, abdominal palpation, and rectal/neuro screen when appropriate.
  3. Determine if the Pet is straining, blocked, or unable to urinate.
  4. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  5. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  6. Route for CMO approval when required.
  7. Escalate obstruction concerns immediately.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatments, and follow-up plan.

Protocol Summary

Blocked cat: Profile Only
Straining to urinate: X-rays allowed
No urination for 48 hrs: X-rays allowed
PU/PD: CBC + Profile with Electrolytes
UA/culture/cystogram/US: CMO approval required
