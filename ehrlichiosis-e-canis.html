Diagnostic Algorithm

Ehrlichiosis (E. canis) Infections

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with suspected or known E. canis exposure.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Ehrlichiosis testing is not listed as an automatic diagnostic trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. SNAP 3Dx, PCR, IFA titer, repeat CBC monitoring, and confirmatory infectious disease testing require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless another approved protocol trigger applies.

LEVEL 1
SUSPECTED EHRLICHIOSIS / E. CANIS CONCERN

History

  • Tick exposure or travel history
  • Current tick prevention status
  • Lethargy, anorexia, weight loss, or fever
  • Lameness or shifting-leg discomfort
  • Bleeding, bruising, petechiae, or epistaxis
  • Prior SNAP, PCR, IFA, or treatment history

Physical Exam

  • Temperature and vital parameters
  • Mucous membrane color
  • Petechiae or ecchymoses
  • Lymph node assessment
  • Joint pain or lameness
  • Anterior uveitis or neurologic signs

Protocol Check

  • Pale mucous membranes?
  • Bleeding concern?
  • Not eating trigger met?
  • Fever/systemic illness?
  • Need SNAP/PCR/IFA?
  • CMO approval needed?
↓

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does the Pet Meet an Existing Protocol Trigger?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin or pyometra concern
  • ☐ Respiratory distress

CBC + SHORT PROFILE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and less than 1 year old
  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and over 8 years old
  • ☐ Not eating for 72 hrs and 2–8 years old
  • ☐ Another listed protocol trigger applies

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not run SNAP 3Dx, PCR, IFA, CBC monitoring, or confirmatory infectious disease testing automatically.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before proceeding.
↓
STEP 2

If E. canis Testing Is Requested

E. canis screening or confirmation should be treated as above-protocol testing unless specifically approved. Submit the Diagnostic & Requisition request and wait for CMO approval before proceeding.

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ SNAP 3Dx / 4Dx style testing
  • ☐ E. canis PCR
  • ☐ IFA titer
  • ☐ Repeat CBC monitoring solely for ehrlichiosis
  • ☐ Additional tick-borne disease testing

SUPPORTIVE CARE / DOCUMENTATION

  • ☐ Document clinical signs and exposure history
  • ☐ Review tick prevention status
  • ☐ Escalate concerning signs promptly
  • ☐ Follow CMO-approved plan once received
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Pale Mucous Membranes

CBC is protocol-approved. Do not automatically add SNAP, PCR, IFA, reticulocyte count, chemistry, or other diagnostics without CMO approval.

Bleeding / Bruising Concern

CBC is protocol-approved when bleeding is a presenting complaint. Coagulation testing, infectious disease confirmation, and expanded panels require CMO approval.

Not Eating Trigger Met

If the Pet meets the age and duration criteria for not eating, CBC and Short Profile are protocol-approved. Tick-borne disease testing still requires CMO approval.

Respiratory Distress

CBC is protocol-approved, and x-rays are protocol-approved if respiratory distress is present. Additional infectious disease testing requires CMO approval.

Positive Prior E. canis Result

Document the prior result, clinical signs, and treatment history. Repeat SNAP, PCR, IFA, or CBC monitoring for ehrlichiosis requires CMO approval.

No Current Clinical Signs

If no protocol trigger is present, do not run automatic testing. Educate on tick prevention and submit a request if screening or confirmation is desired.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

SNAP 3Dx/4Dx testing, PCR, IFA titer, repeat CBC monitoring solely for ehrlichiosis, expanded tick-borne disease testing, chemistry profile, urinalysis, coagulation profile, imaging, referral diagnostics, or other testing above the Diagnostic Testing Protocols require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Treatment / Monitoring Direction

Treatment decisions, doxycycline protocols, repeat testing, and long-term monitoring should follow the CMO-approved plan. Document clinical signs, lab abnormalities, tick prevention, treatment instructions, and follow-up plan.

Critter Clues

  • Ehrlichiosis signs can wax and wane.
  • Clinical signs may include lethargy, lameness, bleeding, weight loss, lymph node enlargement, anorexia, uveitis, or neurologic signs.
  • Lab abnormalities may include anemia, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, pancytopenia, or hyperglobulinemia.
  • SNAP tests may stay positive long-term after treatment.
  • E. canis-specific diagnostics require CMO approval unless explicitly authorized.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain history and perform physical exam.
  2. Document tick exposure, prevention status, and clinical signs.
  3. Check whether an existing protocol trigger applies.
  4. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  5. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for SNAP, PCR, IFA, repeat CBC monitoring, or other above-protocol diagnostics.
  6. Route for CMO approval when required.
  7. Follow the approved treatment or monitoring plan.
  8. Document findings, approvals, treatments, education, and outcomes.

Protocol Summary

Pale mucous membranes: CBC
Bleeding concern: CBC
Approved not-eating trigger: CBC + Short Profile
SNAP/PCR/IFA: CMO approval required
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
