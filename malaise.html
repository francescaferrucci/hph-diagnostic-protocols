Diagnostic Algorithm

Malaise

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets who are lethargic, weak, quiet, or “not themselves.”

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Malaise is a clinical sign, not a diagnosis, and is not listed as an automatic diagnostic trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically run CBC/diff, chemistry panel, electrolytes, urinalysis, fecal testing, 3Dx, FeLV/FIV, T4, radiographs, ECG, ultrasound, SNAP cPL, tick titers, ACTH stimulation test, or other diagnostics unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
MALAISE / LETHARGY / NOT DOING WELL

History

  • Onset, duration, and progression
  • Appetite and water intake
  • Vomiting, diarrhea, coughing, sneezing, urinary signs, or pain
  • Possible toxin, medication, diet, or environmental exposure
  • Travel, tick exposure, boarding, daycare, or sick contacts
  • Prior illness, chronic disease, or recent treatment

Physical Exam

  • Temperature and full vital parameters
  • Hydration and mucous membrane color
  • Pain assessment
  • Thoracic auscultation
  • Abdominal palpation
  • Neurologic, orthopedic, skin, oral, and ocular screen

Protocol Check

  • Respiratory distress?
  • Pale mucous membranes?
  • Bleeding, toxin, or pyometra concern?
  • Not eating trigger met?
  • Vomiting or diarrhea trigger met?
  • Trauma, tumor suspect, straining, or other x-ray trigger?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does Another Hannah Protocol Trigger Apply?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

CBC + SHORT PROFILE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and less than 1 year old
  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and over 8 years old
  • ☐ Not eating for 72 hrs and 2–8 years old
  • ☐ Productive vomiting for more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Dog/Cat over 1 year with diarrhea for more than 72 hrs

X-RAYS APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Tumor suspect, 3 views
  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Obvious broken bone or dislocated joint
  • ☐ Vomiting more than 48 hrs, non-productive vomiting, or suspected foreign body
  • ☐ Straining/no urination or defecation trigger

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not run automatic diagnostics for malaise alone.
  • ☐ Document history, exam findings, and stability assessment.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before proceeding.
↓
STEP 2

Localize the Problem

Malaise can evolve into a more specific presenting complaint. Reassess frequently and route to the appropriate protocol if additional signs develop, such as anorexia, vomiting, diarrhea, coughing, lameness, pale mucous membranes, urinary signs, fever, or neurologic changes.

LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Malaise Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for malaise alone. Perform history, physical exam, stability assessment, documentation, monitoring, and submit Diagnostic & Requisition request if diagnostics are needed.

Malaise + Not Eating

If the Pet meets the age and duration criteria for not eating, CBC and Short Profile are protocol-approved. If the not-eating criteria are not met, diagnostics require CMO approval.

Malaise + Respiratory Distress

CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved if respiratory distress is present. Escalate promptly due to patient risk.

Malaise + Pale Mucous Membranes

CBC is protocol-approved. Additional anemia workup, chemistry, urinalysis, imaging, or infectious disease testing requires CMO approval unless another protocol trigger applies.

Malaise + Vomiting or Diarrhea

Follow the Acute Vomiting or Diarrhea workflow if those triggers apply. Run only the specific protocol-approved diagnostics for that presentation.

Malaise + Trauma, Tumor, or Mobility Concern

X-rays are protocol-approved only when a listed x-ray trigger applies, such as vehicular trauma, obvious fracture/dislocation, paresis/paralysis, tumor suspect, or other listed x-ray trigger.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

CBC/diff when no protocol trigger applies, chemistry panel, electrolytes, urinalysis, fecal testing, 3Dx/4Dx, FeLV/FIV, T4, blood parasite evaluation, radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, ECG, ophthalmic tonometry, ultrasound, SNAP cPL, tick titers, ACTH stimulation test, advanced imaging, referral diagnostics, or any other broad malaise workup require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Reassessment / Escalation

If malaise persists, worsens, or becomes associated with anorexia, fever, vomiting, diarrhea, respiratory distress, pale mucous membranes, collapse, pain, neurologic signs, urinary signs, or weight loss, reassess and route to the appropriate workflow. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Malaise is a sign, not a disease.
  • A thorough history and physical exam are essential.
  • Do not start with a broad automatic baseline database.
  • Route to the appropriate workflow when a specific clinical sign emerges.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history.
  2. Perform complete physical exam and stability assessment.
  3. Identify whether a specific protocol trigger applies.
  4. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  5. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  6. Route for CMO approval when required.
  7. Monitor closely and reassess for emerging localizing signs.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatments, and plan.

Protocol Summary

Malaise alone: No automatic diagnostics
Respiratory distress / pale MM / bleeding / toxin / pyometra: CBC
Approved not-eating, vomiting, or diarrhea trigger: CBC + Short Profile
Listed x-ray trigger: X-rays allowed
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
