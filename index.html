Diagnostic Algorithm

Elevated Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)

Protocol-aligned workflow for confirming and escalating an elevated ALP finding.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Elevated ALP is a laboratory finding, not a stand-alone presenting complaint. Begin with history, physical exam, and review/confirmation of the existing lab result. Additional bloodwork, bile acids, urinalysis, ultrasound, CT/MRI, culture, cytology/FNA, biopsy, infectious disease testing, or referral diagnostics require a Diagnostic & Requisition form and doctor approval unless the patient also meets a listed Hannah presenting-complaint testing trigger.

📋 Open PetNurse Checklist
LEVEL 1
ELEVATED ALKALINE PHOSPHATASE (ALP)

History

Medication history, age, pregnancy/lactation status, trauma or orthopedic surgery, appetite, PU/PD, vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, weight loss, and prior liver/endocrine disease.

Physical Exam

Assess hydration, body condition, abdominal pain or distension, hepatomegaly, jaundice, fever, endocrine signs, and evidence of bone or systemic disease.

Review Labs

Confirm ALP value, trend, concurrent ALT/AST/GGT/bilirubin changes, sample quality, hemolysis/lipemia, and whether chemistry was already authorized under protocol.

↓

Confirm Elevation Before Escalating

Rule out pre-analytical error, sample quality issues, lipemia/hemolysis, transcription error, and isolated mild elevations. If stable and no protocol trigger applies, document findings and route the next-step request for doctor approval.

Protocol-Approved Automatic Testing

  • Use only if the patient meets a current presenting-complaint trigger, such as age/appetite criteria, productive vomiting >48 hr, diarrhea criteria, PU/PD, pale mucous membranes, respiratory distress, bleeding/toxin/pyometra, or blocked-cat protocol.
  • If no trigger applies, do not add CBC, chemistry, urinalysis, bile acids, T4, imaging, or infectious testing without approval.

Physiologic / Non-Hepatic Possibilities

  • Young/growing patient
  • Late pregnancy or lactation
  • Recent bone fracture or orthopedic surgery
  • Glucocorticoid or anticonvulsant medications
  • Hyperthyroidism in cats
LEVEL 2

Doctor-Approved Diagnostic Pathways

Hepatocellular Pattern

Doctor-approved options may include complete chemistry review, urinalysis, bile acids/hepatic function testing, abdominal ultrasound, and liver sampling if indicated.

Cholangitis / Cholangiohepatitis

Doctor-approved options may include abdominal ultrasound, bile acids, bile/urine culture, infectious testing, and biopsy when clinically justified.

Biliary Obstruction

Doctor-approved options may include abdominal ultrasound, bile acids, ERCP or exploratory surgery planning, and referral when indicated.

Portosystemic Shunt (PSS)

Doctor-approved options may include bile acids, ammonia level, Doppler ultrasound, CT angiography, and specialty referral.

Neoplasia

If tumor suspect, protocol supports 3-view radiographs. Other staging, ultrasound, cytology/FNA, CT, or biopsy should be doctor-approved.

Non-Hepatic Cause

Treat or investigate the underlying non-hepatic condition as directed; recheck ALP trend only when approved or bundled with an authorized protocol trigger.

LEVEL 3

Advanced / Specialized Diagnostics

Advanced imaging, ERCP/endoscopy, liver biopsy, laparoscopy/open surgery, CT/MRI, culture, cytology/FNA, and internal medicine referral are not automatic Pet Nurse tests. Use Diagnostic & Requisition workflow and doctor approval.

Monitoring

Trend ALP and related liver values only as part of an approved recheck plan. Document patient response, medications, clinical signs, and whether the elevation is improving, stable, or worsening.

Critter Clues

  • ALP is not specific to liver disease.
  • Confirm the elevation before escalating.
  • Patient age, medications, physiologic status, and bone disease matter.
  • Hepatic vs. extrahepatic source determines the next doctor-approved path.
  • Advanced testing should identify the cause, not simply repeat tests.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain history and perform complete physical exam.
  2. Confirm ALP elevation and review all existing lab results.
  3. Check whether a Hannah protocol trigger allows automatic testing.
  4. If no trigger applies, document and submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  5. After doctor approval, support selected Level 2 diagnostics.
  6. Escalate advanced diagnostics only when directed.
  7. Communicate findings and plan with the pet parent as directed.
  8. Document all findings, approvals, tests, treatments, and outcomes.

Abbreviations

ALP = alkaline phosphatase • US = ultrasound • ERCP = endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography • CT = computed tomography • MRI = magnetic resonance imaging • FNA = fine needle aspirate • PSS = portosystemic shunt
