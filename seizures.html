Seizure Diagnostic Workflow

Aligned to Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols for PetNurse decision support.

PetNurse Workflow Tool
Protocol control: Seizures are not listed as a pre-approved presenting complaint on the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Complete the approved baseline steps first. Any additional bloodwork, imaging, specialized infectious disease testing, ECG, MRI, CSF tap, or other diagnostics require a Diagnostic & Requisition Form and approval by Dr. Horner or Dr. Phillips before proceeding, unless a veterinarian has already directly authorized the test.
Start
Seizure / Suspected Seizure Event
  1. Assess and stabilize patient.
  2. Notify veterinarian if active seizure, cluster seizures, status epilepticus, toxin concern, severe trauma, respiratory distress, pale mucous membranes, or other emergency signs are present.
  3. Obtain focused seizure history: onset, duration, frequency, recovery, possible toxin exposure, medications, diet, trauma, and video if available.
↓
Level 1 — Initial Assessment
  • History
  • Full physical exam
  • Cardiac exam
  • Neurologic exam
  • Differentiate generalized vs. focal seizure when possible
↓
Approved Baseline Diagnostics First
  • CBC as baseline bloodwork.
  • Short chemistry / internal organ screen when clinically indicated and veterinarian-approved as part of baseline evaluation.
  • Urinalysis only when included in the patient’s age/status protocol or veterinarian-approved.
  • FeLV/FIV for cats only when age/status protocol applies or veterinarian-approved.
  • Fecal exam only when age/status protocol applies or veterinarian-approved.

Do not add unapproved blood tests before completing the required baseline pathway.

↓
Decision Point — Diagnosis or Clear Veterinarian Direction?
Yes: Follow veterinarian treatment plan. Begin/adjust anticonvulsant therapy only as directed. Monitor therapeutic drug levels when prescribed.
No: Do not proceed directly to advanced testing. Complete a Diagnostic & Requisition Form and submit to Dr. Horner or Dr. Phillips for approval.
↓
Requires Approval
Level 2 — Advanced Database After Approval
  • Thyroid panel
  • Bile acids
  • Lead/toxin testing
  • Cholinesterase
  • ECG
  • Radiographs
  • Tick titers
  • Toxoplasmosis
  • Distemper antibody IFA — serum
  • Distemper antigen IFA — blood smear or conjunctival smear
  • Distemper antibody on CSF
↓
Diagnosis Reached
  • Communicate plan to team and pet parent.
  • Document diagnostics, approval, treatment, monitoring plan, and recheck timing.
  • Monitor medication response and side effects.
No Diagnosis After Level 2

Veterinarian reviews results. If still unresolved, obtain approval before Level 3 neurodiagnostics.

↓
Level 3 — Advanced Neurodiagnostics After Approval
  • CSF tap
  • MRI
  • Distemper antibody on CSF when indicated
  • Specialist referral or advanced care plan as directed by veterinarian
PetNurse Reminders
  • Stabilization and safety come first.
  • Always document seizure description and recovery period.
  • Flag toxin exposure, trauma, cluster seizures, and prolonged events immediately.
  • Confirm approval before any testing outside baseline protocol.
Approval Rule

For any diagnostic not already approved by Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols, complete the Diagnostic & Requisition Form and send to Dr. Horner or Dr. Phillips for approval before moving forward.
