Diagnostic Algorithm

Seizures

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with seizure activity, seizure-like episodes, or neurologic events.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Seizures are not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically run CBC, chemistry, urinalysis, thyroid panel, bile acids, toxin testing, ECG, radiographs, tick titers, infectious disease testing, CSF tap, MRI, or referral diagnostics unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
SEIZURE / SUSPECTED SEIZURE EVENT

History

  • Onset, duration, frequency, and recovery period
  • Generalized vs. focal activity if known
  • Video of event if available
  • Possible toxin, medication, or diet exposure
  • Trauma, collapse, or respiratory signs
  • Prior seizures or anticonvulsant use

Initial Assessment

  • Stabilize patient first
  • Temperature and full vital parameters
  • Mucous membrane color
  • Cardiac and neurologic exam
  • Post-ictal status and mentation
  • Assess for active seizure or cluster activity

Emergency Screen

  • Active seizure?
  • Cluster seizures?
  • Status epilepticus?
  • Toxin concern?
  • Severe trauma?
  • Respiratory distress or pale mucous membranes?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Is the Pet Actively Seizing or Unstable?

YES — STABILIZE / ESCALATE

  • ☐ Protect patient from injury.
  • ☐ Minimize stimulation.
  • ☐ Notify medical leadership immediately.
  • ☐ Do not delay stabilization for diagnostics.
  • ☐ Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

NO — CONTINUE TRIAGE

  • ☐ Obtain detailed event history.
  • ☐ Perform physical, cardiac, and neurologic exam.
  • ☐ Screen for toxin, trauma, respiratory distress, pale mucous membranes, or other protocol triggers.

STEP 2

Does Another Hannah Protocol Trigger Apply?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

X-RAYS APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Obvious broken bone or dislocated joint
  • ☐ Paresis/paralysis
  • ☐ Respiratory distress

SEIZURE ALONE

  • ☐ No automatic diagnostics are approved for seizure alone.
  • ☐ Document event details, neuro status, and recovery.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Seizure Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for seizure activity alone. Document event description, duration, recovery period, possible exposure, physical exam, and neurologic findings.

Seizure + Toxin Concern

CBC is protocol-approved when toxin concern is present. Toxin panels, lead testing, cholinesterase, or additional bloodwork require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Seizure + Pale Mucous Membranes

CBC is protocol-approved. Chemistry, bile acids, thyroid panel, urinalysis, imaging, or infectious disease testing require CMO approval unless another protocol trigger applies.

Seizure + Respiratory Distress

CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved if respiratory distress is present. Stabilize first and escalate promptly.

Seizure + Trauma or Paresis/Paralysis

X-rays are protocol-approved for vehicular trauma, obvious fracture/dislocation, or paresis/paralysis. Advanced neurodiagnostics require CMO approval.

Recurrent or Unexplained Seizures

Additional seizure workup may be clinically appropriate, but diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

CBC when no protocol trigger applies, chemistry/internal organ screen, electrolytes, urinalysis, thyroid panel, bile acids, lead or toxin testing, cholinesterase, ECG, radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, tick titers, toxoplasmosis testing, distemper antibody or antigen testing, infectious disease testing, advanced neurologic diagnostics, specialist referral, or other seizure workup require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Monitoring / Escalation

Escalate immediately for active seizure, cluster seizures, status epilepticus, prolonged recovery, toxin concern, trauma, collapse, respiratory distress, pale mucous membranes, abnormal vitals, or worsening neurologic status. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Stabilization and patient safety come first.
  • Document seizure description, duration, recovery, and video if available.
  • Flag toxin exposure, trauma, clusters, prolonged events, and respiratory distress immediately.
  • Seizures alone do not authorize automatic diagnostics.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Assess and stabilize patient.
  2. Notify leadership immediately for active seizure, cluster seizures, status epilepticus, toxin concern, trauma, or instability.
  3. Obtain focused seizure history.
  4. Perform physical, cardiac, and neurologic exam.
  5. Determine whether another protocol trigger applies.
  6. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  7. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for seizure diagnostics above protocol.
  8. Document findings, approvals, treatment plan, monitoring, and recheck instructions.

Protocol Summary

Seizure alone: No automatic diagnostics
Toxin / pale MM / bleeding / respiratory distress: CBC allowed
Respiratory distress: X-rays allowed
Trauma / paresis / paralysis: X-rays allowed
Neurodiagnostics: CMO approval required
