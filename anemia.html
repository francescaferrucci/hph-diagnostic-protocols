Diagnostic Algorithm

Anemia

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with pale mucous membranes or suspected anemia.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Under Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols, automatic testing for a Dog or Cat with pale mucous membranes is limited to CBC. CBC is also protocol-approved when bleeding, toxin exposure, or pyometra is suspected. Diagnostics beyond these protocol triggers require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
SUSPECTED ANEMIA / PALE MUCOUS MEMBRANES

History

  • Current medications
  • Known toxin exposure
  • Bleeding or trauma history
  • Vomiting, diarrhea, melena, or blood loss
  • Prior illness or chronic disease
  • Appetite, energy, collapse, or weakness

Physical Exam

  • Mucous membrane color
  • Capillary refill time
  • Heart rate and respiratory rate
  • Pulse quality
  • Abdominal distension or pain
  • Evidence of bleeding, bruising, or parasites

Confirm Protocol Trigger

  • Pale mucous membranes?
  • Bleeding concern?
  • Toxin concern?
  • Pyometra concern?
  • Respiratory distress?
  • Trauma or tumor suspect?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Is a CBC Allowed by Protocol?

YES — CBC APPROVED

  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

NO CLEAR TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not add CBC or additional diagnostics automatically.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before proceeding.
↓
STEP 2

Are Additional Diagnostics Needed?

CBC may confirm anemia, but additional diagnostics to classify anemia or identify the cause are above the basic Diagnostic Testing Protocols unless another approved trigger applies.

X-RAYS MAY BE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Tumor suspect, 3 views
  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Other listed x-ray trigger applies

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Reticulocyte count
  • ☐ Blood smear evaluation
  • ☐ Chemistry/internal organ screen
  • ☐ Urinalysis
  • ☐ FeLV/FIV or heartworm testing
  • ☐ Coagulation testing
  • ☐ Ultrasound, biopsy, PCR, endoscopy, or referral diagnostics
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Pale Mucous Membranes

Protocol-approved diagnostic: CBC only. Document mucous membrane color, CRT, vitals, hydration status, and clinical concern.

Bleeding Concern

CBC is protocol-approved. Additional coagulation testing, imaging, ultrasound, or specialty diagnostics require CMO approval unless another listed protocol trigger applies.

Toxin Concern

CBC is protocol-approved. Toxicology, chemistry profile, urinalysis, coagulation testing, or additional diagnostics require CMO approval unless specifically directed.

Pyometra Concern

CBC is protocol-approved. Any additional diagnostics or imaging should follow protocol triggers or require CMO approval.

Respiratory Distress

CBC is protocol-approved, and x-rays are protocol-approved if respiratory distress is present. Escalate immediately due to patient risk.

Trauma or Tumor Suspect

X-rays are protocol-approved for vehicular trauma, obvious broken bone or dislocated joint, or tumor suspect. Tumor suspect requires 3 views.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Reticulocyte count, blood smear evaluation, slide agglutination, urinalysis, chemistry/internal organ screen, FeLV/FIV, heartworm testing, fecal testing, coagulation profile, ACT, PT/PTT, BMBT, von Willebrand testing, PCR testing, toxicology, iron levels, abdominal ultrasound, endoscopy, biopsy, bone marrow aspirate/biopsy, or referral diagnostics require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Monitoring / Escalation

If the Pet is weak, collapsing, has pale or white mucous membranes, abnormal vitals, active bleeding, respiratory distress, or worsening clinical status, escalate immediately while staying within protocol-approved diagnostics unless CMO approval is obtained.

Critter Clues

  • Anemia is a finding, not a diagnosis.
  • Confirm pale mucous membranes and patient stability first.
  • CBC is allowed for pale mucous membranes, bleeding, toxin, or pyometra concern.
  • Do not automatically add reticulocyte count, blood smear, chemistry, UA, or imaging.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain history and perform physical exam.
  2. Assess mucous membrane color, CRT, vitals, pulse quality, and stability.
  3. Determine whether a protocol trigger applies.
  4. Run CBC only when allowed by protocol.
  5. Use x-rays only when a listed x-ray trigger applies.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatments, and monitoring plan.

Protocol Summary

Pale mucous membranes: CBC
Bleeding/toxin/pyometra: CBC
Respiratory distress: CBC; x-rays allowed
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
