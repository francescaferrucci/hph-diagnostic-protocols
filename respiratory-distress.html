Diagnostic Algorithm

Respiratory Distress

Protocol-aligned emergency workflow for Pets with difficulty breathing, increased effort, cyanosis, collapse, or open-mouth breathing.

Protocol-approved
CBC + X-rays

Testing Guardrail

Respiratory distress is specifically listed in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Protocol-approved diagnostics are CBC and x-rays. Additional diagnostics including chemistry, blood gas, pulse oximetry, blood pressure, ECG, ultrasound, thoracocentesis fluid analysis, infectious disease testing, echocardiogram, CT, airway wash, or referral diagnostics require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless emergently directed.

LEVEL 1
RESPIRATORY DISTRESS

Immediate Triage

  • Open-mouth breathing
  • Increased respiratory effort
  • Cyanosis or pale mucous membranes
  • Collapse or weakness
  • Severe coughing or choking concern
  • Unable to tolerate handling

Stabilize First

  • Minimize stress and handling
  • Provide oxygen if indicated
  • Keep Pet calm and cool
  • Notify medical leadership immediately
  • Do not delay stabilization for diagnostics
  • Proceed only as patient allows

Focused History

  • Onset and duration
  • Coughing, gagging, choking, trauma, toxin, or heat exposure
  • Known cardiac or respiratory disease
  • Recent vomiting or aspiration concern
  • Travel, boarding, infectious exposure
  • Current medications

LEVEL 2

Likely Emergency Categories

Upper Airway Concern

May include obstruction, laryngeal paralysis, tracheal collapse, foreign body, swelling, or brachycephalic airway crisis. Stabilize, minimize stress, and perform protocol-approved CBC and x-rays when patient can tolerate them.

Lower Airway / Pulmonary Concern

May include pneumonia, aspiration, pulmonary edema, asthma-like crisis, contusions, pneumothorax, pleural effusion, or neoplasia. CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved.

Cardiac Concern

May include congestive heart failure, arrhythmia, murmur-associated disease, or pulmonary edema. CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved. ECG, echo, NT-proBNP, and cardiac workup require CMO approval.

Trauma Concern

Vehicular trauma and respiratory distress both approve x-rays. Escalate immediately and handle carefully to avoid worsening distress.

Pale Mucous Membranes

CBC is protocol-approved for pale mucous membranes. If respiratory distress is also present, x-rays are also protocol-approved.

Stable After Triage

If the Pet is no longer in distress, continue monitoring and route to the most appropriate workflow for the primary sign, such as Coughing, Sneezing/Nasal Discharge, Malaise, or Ascites.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Chemistry, electrolytes, blood gas, lactate, pulse oximetry as diagnostic workup, blood pressure, ECG, echocardiogram, NT-proBNP, thoracic ultrasound, CT, airway wash/BAL, cytology, culture, infectious disease testing, fungal testing, heartworm testing, thoracocentesis fluid analysis, specialty referral, or any diagnostics above CBC and x-rays require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Escalation

Escalate immediately for open-mouth breathing, cyanosis, collapse, severe increased effort, rapidly worsening signs, oxygen dependence, suspected airway obstruction, suspected pleural space disease, severe trauma, shock, or inability to safely handle the Pet.

Critter Clues

  • Respiratory distress is an emergency.
  • Stabilize before diagnostics whenever possible.
  • Minimize stress and handling.
  • CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved for respiratory distress.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Recognize respiratory distress immediately.
  2. Minimize stress and provide oxygen if indicated.
  3. Notify medical leadership immediately.
  4. Obtain focused history only as patient allows.
  5. Run protocol-approved CBC and x-rays when stable enough.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, stabilization, diagnostics, response, and plan.

Protocol Summary

Respiratory distress: CBC + x-rays allowed
Pale mucous membranes: CBC allowed
Trauma: x-rays allowed
Cardiac/airway/advanced diagnostics: CMO approval required
Stabilization: always first
