Diagnostic Algorithm

Growth / Lump

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets presenting with a mass, swelling, lump, or growth.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

A growth or lump does not automatically authorize FNA, cytology, CBC/diff, internal organ screen, urinalysis, biopsy, histopathology, ultrasound, or oncology diagnostics. Under the Diagnostic Testing Protocols, x-rays are protocol-approved when the Pet is tumor suspect, with 3 views required. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
GROWTH / LUMP IDENTIFIED

History

  • When the lump was first noticed
  • Change in size, shape, color, or texture
  • Pain, bleeding, ulceration, discharge, or itching
  • Prior lumps, removals, cytology, or biopsy results
  • Appetite, energy, weight loss, coughing, or systemic signs
  • Trauma, bite wound, vaccine site, or injection history

Physical Exam

  • Location and number of masses
  • Size measurement in centimeters
  • Firmness, mobility, attachment, and depth
  • Pain, heat, ulceration, bleeding, or drainage
  • Regional lymph node assessment
  • Full body mass check

Protocol Check

  • Is this tumor suspect?
  • Are 3-view radiographs indicated by protocol?
  • Is there respiratory distress?
  • Is there trauma or obvious bone involvement?
  • Is FNA/cytology being requested?
  • Is CMO approval needed?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does a Listed Protocol Trigger Apply?

TUMOR SUSPECT

  • ☐ 3-view radiographs are protocol-approved.
  • ☐ Document why the mass is tumor suspect.
  • ☐ Document mass size, location, and characteristics.

OTHER X-RAY TRIGGERS

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Obvious broken bone or dislocated joint
  • ☐ Other listed x-ray trigger applies

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not perform FNA, cytology, labwork, biopsy, histopathology, or advanced imaging automatically.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before proceeding.
↓
STEP 2

If Further Mass Workup Is Needed

FNA, cytology, biopsy, histopathology, oncology workup, and staging diagnostics are above the Diagnostic Testing Protocols unless specifically approved. Submit the request and wait for CMO approval before proceeding.

LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Mass Not Clearly Tumor Suspect

Document mass characteristics and monitor or escalate as directed. No automatic diagnostics are approved solely because a lump is present.

Tumor Suspect

Protocol-approved diagnostic: 3-view radiographs. Additional staging, FNA, cytology, labwork, ultrasound, or biopsy requires CMO approval.

Growth with Respiratory Distress

CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved if respiratory distress is present. Escalate promptly because this may indicate significant systemic or thoracic disease.

Growth with Trauma or Bone Concern

X-rays are protocol-approved for vehicular trauma or obvious broken bone/dislocated joint. Additional diagnostics require CMO approval unless another trigger applies.

Bleeding or Pale Mucous Membranes

CBC is protocol-approved if bleeding concern or pale mucous membranes are present. Coagulation testing, cytology, biopsy, or staging require CMO approval.

No Current Clinical Concern

Measure, photograph if appropriate, document, and monitor as directed. Submit request if further diagnostics are desired.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Fine needle aspirate, cytology, CBC/diff when no protocol trigger applies, internal organ screen, urinalysis, biopsy, histopathology, abdominal ultrasound, oncology staging, lymph node aspirate, advanced imaging, referral diagnostics, chemotherapy planning, or surgical removal for diagnostic purposes require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless separately authorized.

Monitoring / Escalation

Reassess and escalate if the mass grows quickly, becomes painful, ulcerated, bleeding, infected, fixed, firm, associated with weight loss, respiratory signs, pale mucous membranes, or other systemic illness. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Not all lumps are neoplastic.
  • Document size, location, consistency, mobility, and duration.
  • 3-view radiographs are protocol-approved when tumor suspect.
  • FNA, cytology, biopsy, and histopathology require CMO approval.
  • Fast-growing, ulcerated, fixed, or painful masses should be escalated.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain history and assess lump characteristics.
  2. Perform physical exam and full body mass check.
  3. Measure and document mass size, location, consistency, and mobility.
  4. Determine whether tumor suspect or another protocol trigger applies.
  5. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for FNA, cytology, biopsy, histopathology, or staging.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatments, and plan.

Protocol Summary

Tumor suspect: 3-view radiographs allowed
Respiratory distress: CBC + x-rays allowed
Pale MM / bleeding: CBC allowed
FNA/cytology/biopsy/histopath: CMO approval required
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
