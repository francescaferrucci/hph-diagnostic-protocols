Diagnostic Algorithm

Acute Vomiting

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets presenting with acute vomiting.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Acute vomiting is a clinical sign, not a diagnosis. Start with history, physical exam, hydration assessment, and stability check. Automatic testing is limited to Hannah-approved triggers. Any testing outside these triggers requires a Diagnostic & Requisition request and doctor approval.

LEVEL 1
ACUTE VOMITING

History

  • Onset, duration, frequency, and character of vomiting
  • Productive vs. non-productive vomiting
  • Diet history and recent food changes
  • Access to garbage, toys, bones, fabric, or foreign material
  • Medication or toxin exposure
  • Vaccination status
  • Other Pets affected

Physical Exam

  • Hydration status
  • Vital parameters
  • Mucous membrane color
  • Abdominal pain or distension
  • Oral exam
  • Neurologic status
  • Signs of shock or systemic illness

Stability Check

  • Is the Pet stable?
  • Is vomiting productive or non-productive?
  • Has vomiting continued for more than 48 hours?
  • Is foreign body suspected?
  • Is immediate stabilization needed?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Is the Pet Acute, Severe, or Life-Threatening?

  • ☐ Shock or abnormal vitals
  • ☐ Severe dehydration
  • ☐ Severe abdominal pain or distension
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Repeated unsuccessful vomiting or retching

If unstable, stabilize first and involve the doctor immediately.

↓
STEP 2

Does the Pet Meet a Hannah Testing Trigger?

PRODUCTIVE VOMITING > 48 HOURS

  • ☐ CBC
  • ☐ Short Profile
  • ☐ Document duration and clinical signs

X-RAY APPROVED TRIGGERS

  • ☐ Vomiting more than 48 hours
  • ☐ Non-productive vomiting
  • ☐ Suspected foreign body

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not add bloodwork, UA, fecal, SAA, cPL, bile acids, cortisol, ultrasound, or other diagnostics.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ Route to Dr. Horner or Dr. Phillips for approval.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Stable Pet, Vomiting Less Than 48 Hours

Supportive care and monitoring as directed. No automatic CBC, chemistry, urinalysis, fecal testing, SAA, cPL, or imaging unless another Hannah-approved trigger is present.

Productive Vomiting More Than 48 Hours

Protocol-approved diagnostics: CBC and Short Profile. Document vomiting duration, hydration status, physical exam findings, and doctor-directed treatment plan.

Vomiting More Than 48 Hours

Abdominal radiographs are protocol-approved. CBC and Short Profile are approved if vomiting is productive for more than 48 hours.

Non-Productive Vomiting

Abdominal radiographs are protocol-approved. Notify the doctor promptly due to concern for obstruction, bloat, foreign body, or other urgent causes.

Suspected Foreign Body

Abdominal radiographs are protocol-approved. Additional diagnostics such as contrast study, ultrasound, endoscopy, or surgery require doctor direction.

Pale Mucous Membranes, Toxin, or Bleeding Concern

CBC is protocol-approved if pale mucous membranes, toxin exposure, or bleeding concern is present. Escalate to doctor for next steps.

LEVEL 3

CMO-Approved Diagnostics Only

Full chemistry profile, urinalysis, fecal testing, SAA, SNAP cPL, bile acids, TLI, cortisol, blood pressure, lactate, ultrasound, contrast studies, endoscopy, biopsy, toxicology testing, exploratory surgery, or referral diagnostics require Diagnostic & Requisition approval unless separately authorized by the doctor.

Reassessment / Escalation

If the Pet worsens, fails to respond, continues vomiting, becomes painful, develops abnormal vitals, or has new concerning findings, reassess immediately and escalate to the doctor.

Critter Clues

  • Acute vomiting is a sign, not a diagnosis.
  • Confirm whether vomiting is productive or non-productive.
  • Duration matters: more than 48 hours changes testing eligibility.
  • Non-productive vomiting can indicate an urgent condition.
  • Foreign body concern qualifies for radiographs.
  • Do not add extra diagnostics without approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history.
  2. Perform physical exam and stability assessment.
  3. Identify duration of vomiting.
  4. Determine productive vs. non-productive vomiting.
  5. Screen for foreign body, toxin, pale mucous membranes, pain, or shock.
  6. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  7. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition form for anything outside protocol.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatments, and response.

Protocol Summary

Productive vomiting >48 hrs: CBC + Short Profile
Vomiting >48 hrs: X-rays allowed
Non-productive vomiting: X-rays allowed
Suspected foreign body: X-rays allowed
Other diagnostics: Doctor approval required
