Diagnostic Algorithm

Otic Disorder

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with ear pain, discharge, odor, head shaking, or suspected otitis.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Otic disorder is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically perform ear cytology, culture/sensitivity, skull radiographs, myringotomy, endocrine testing, allergy testing, food trials, CBC/diff, chemistry, urinalysis, T4, or hyperadrenocorticism testing unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
EAR DISCHARGE / PAIN / HEAD SHAKING

History

  • Onset, duration, and recurrence
  • One ear or both ears
  • Discharge, odor, head shaking, scratching, or pain
  • Recent bathing, swimming, grooming, or foreign body exposure
  • Skin allergies, itching, food changes, or chronic ear history
  • Current medications or prior ear treatments

Physical Exam

  • Assess both ears and pinnae
  • Look for swelling, redness, discharge, odor, pain, or hematoma
  • Assess whether canal can be safely visualized
  • Screen for neurologic signs or head tilt
  • Assess skin for generalized pruritus or allergy signs
  • Check for systemic illness or other protocol triggers

Protocol Check

  • Respiratory distress?
  • Pale mucous membranes?
  • Bleeding, toxin, or pyometra concern?
  • PU/PD or not-eating trigger?
  • Trauma or tumor suspect?
  • CMO approval needed for otic diagnostics?

STEP 2

If Otic Diagnostics Are Needed

Ear cytology, culture/sensitivity, skull radiographs, myringotomy, endocrine testing, and allergy workup are above the Diagnostic Testing Protocols unless specifically approved.

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Ear swab cytology
  • ☐ Culture and sensitivity
  • ☐ Skull radiographs
  • ☐ Myringotomy and culture
  • ☐ T4, CBC/diff, chemistry, urinalysis, endocrine testing
  • ☐ Allergy testing or food trial diagnostics

SUPPORTIVE ACTIONS

  • ☐ Prevent self-trauma if indicated.
  • ☐ Document both ears separately.
  • ☐ Note if ear drum cannot be visualized.
  • ☐ Follow CMO-approved plan once received.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Otic Disorder Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for ear discharge, pain, odor, or head shaking alone. Document exam findings and submit request if diagnostics are needed.

Enlarged Pinnae / Aural Hematoma Concern

Document swelling, pain, laterality, and underlying otitis signs. Diagnostics or procedures above protocol require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Unable to Visualize Ear Drum

Document canal condition and reason visualization is limited. Cleaning under sedation, otoscopic exam, myringotomy, culture, or imaging require CMO approval.

Chronic or Recurrent Otitis

Culture/sensitivity, cytology, endocrine workup, allergy testing, or food trial planning require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Otic Signs + Systemic Trigger

If another protocol trigger applies, follow that workflow. Run only the diagnostics specifically approved by that trigger.

Trauma or Tumor Suspect

X-rays are protocol-approved only when a listed x-ray trigger applies, such as vehicular trauma, obvious fracture/dislocation, or tumor suspect.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Ear swab cytology, culture/sensitivity, skull radiographs, otoscopic exam under sedation, ear cleaning under sedation/anesthesia, myringotomy, T4, CBC/diff when no protocol trigger applies, chemistry, urinalysis, hyperadrenocorticism testing, allergy testing, food trial diagnostics, biopsy, advanced imaging, referral diagnostics, or other otic diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Monitoring / Escalation

Escalate if severe pain, neurologic signs, head tilt, suspected ruptured ear drum, marked swelling, bleeding, inability to examine the ear, worsening discharge, or failure to improve occurs. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Always evaluate both ears.
  • Document discharge, odor, pain, swelling, and canal visibility.
  • Ear cytology guides therapy but is above protocol unless approved.
  • Look for predisposing factors: allergies, endocrine disease, ectoparasites, conformation, and hair.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain history and assess severity.
  2. Perform physical exam of both ears and pinnae.
  3. Document discharge, pain, odor, swelling, and ear drum visibility.
  4. Determine whether another protocol trigger applies.
  5. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for otic diagnostics above protocol.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, approvals, treatment plan, and recheck instructions.

Protocol Summary

Otic disorder alone: No automatic diagnostics
Ear cytology/culture: CMO approval required
Skull rads/myringotomy: CMO approval required
Endocrine/allergy workup: CMO approval required
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
