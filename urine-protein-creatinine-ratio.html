Diagnostic Algorithm

Urine Protein/Creatinine Ratio

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with suspected proteinuria or UPC testing consideration.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Urine Protein/Creatinine Ratio testing is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically run urinalysis, UPC, urine culture, blood pressure, imaging, infectious disease testing, endocrine testing, renal monitoring panels, or advanced diagnostics unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
PROTEINURIA/UPC CONCERN

History

  • Reason proteinuria or UPC is being considered
  • PU/PD, urinary signs, accidents, or straining
  • Appetite, weight change, vomiting, diarrhea, or lethargy
  • Prior kidney disease, hypertension, endocrine disease, or infection
  • Current medications or supplements
  • Prior urinalysis, bloodwork, UPC, or blood pressure results

Physical Exam

  • Hydration status
  • Body weight and body condition
  • Blood pressure concern if previously documented
  • Abdominal palpation
  • Mucous membrane color
  • Signs of systemic illness or urinary discomfort

Protocol Check

  • PU/PD present?
  • Straining to urinate?
  • No urination for 48 hours?
  • Blocked cat?
  • Not eating, vomiting, diarrhea, or pale MM trigger?
  • CMO approval needed for UPC/UA?
↓

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does Another Hannah Protocol Trigger Apply?

PU/PD

  • ☐ CBC
  • ☐ Profile with Electrolytes
  • ☐ Urinalysis and UPC still require CMO approval unless separately authorized.

URINARY X-RAY TRIGGERS

  • ☐ Straining to urinate: x-rays allowed
  • ☐ No urination for 48 hours: x-rays allowed
  • ☐ Blocked cat: Profile Only

OTHER APPROVED TRIGGERS

  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes: CBC
  • ☐ Respiratory distress: CBC + x-rays
  • ☐ Not eating, vomiting, or diarrhea trigger: follow matching workflow

UPC CONCERN ALONE

  • ☐ No automatic diagnostics are approved for UPC concern alone.
  • ☐ Document reason UPC is being considered.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before UPC or UA.
↓
STEP 2

If UPC or Proteinuria Workup Is Needed

UPC interpretation depends on urinalysis, sediment, azotemia status, blood pressure, and possible pre-renal/post-renal causes. These diagnostics are above protocol unless specifically approved through the Diagnostic & Requisition process.

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Complete urinalysis
  • ☐ IDEXX UPC ratio
  • ☐ Urine culture
  • ☐ Blood pressure evaluation
  • ☐ Chest/abdominal imaging outside listed x-ray triggers
  • ☐ Infectious disease, endocrine, renal, or hypertension workup

DOCUMENTATION

  • ☐ Record prior lab abnormalities.
  • ☐ Document urine signs and PU/PD status.
  • ☐ Note any active sediment or infection concern if already known.
  • ☐ Follow CMO-approved plan once received.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

UPC Concern Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for UPC concern alone. Document why proteinuria is suspected and submit Diagnostic & Requisition request if UPC or UA testing is needed.

UPC Concern + PU/PD

PU/PD is protocol-approved for CBC and Profile with Electrolytes. Urinalysis, UPC, urine culture, blood pressure, and additional renal workup require CMO approval unless separately authorized.

UPC Concern + Urinary Straining

X-rays are protocol-approved for straining to urinate. UA, culture, UPC, cystocentesis, and additional urinary diagnostics require CMO approval.

UPC Concern + Blocked Cat

Blocked cat allows Profile Only. Additional diagnostics, including UA or UPC, require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Known Azotemia or Renal Concern

Additional renal diagnostics may be clinically appropriate, but UPC, UA, blood pressure, cultures, and imaging must be routed for CMO approval unless already included in an approved plan.

Monitoring/Recheck

Recheck UPC, urinalysis, blood pressure, renal values, CBC, and electrolytes should follow the CMO-approved monitoring plan.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Complete urinalysis, urine protein/creatinine ratio, urine culture, urine sediment review, blood pressure evaluation, chest or abdominal radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, abdominal ultrasound, renal monitoring panels beyond protocol, CBC when no protocol trigger applies, infectious disease testing, endocrine testing, ACTH stimulation testing, renal biopsy, referral diagnostics, or any additional proteinuria workup require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Monitoring/Escalation

Escalate if the Pet has worsening PU/PD, weight loss, vomiting, anorexia, lethargy, urinary straining, inability to urinate, hypertension concern, worsening renal values, edema, ascites, or other systemic illness. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Proteinuria can be pre-renal, renal, or post-renal.
  • UPC is only one piece of the diagnostic picture.
  • PU/PD allows CBC and Profile with Electrolytes, but not automatic UPC.
  • Urinalysis and culture are above protocol unless approved.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain history and document reason for UPC concern.
  2. Perform physical exam and assess urinary signs, PU/PD, and systemic illness.
  3. Determine whether another protocol trigger applies.
  4. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  5. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for UPC/UA or proteinuria diagnostics above protocol.
  6. Route for CMO approval when required.
  7. Follow the approved monitoring or treatment plan.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatments, and follow-up plan.

Protocol Summary

UPC concern alone: No automatic diagnostics
PU/PD: CBC + Profile with Electrolytes
Straining/no urination: X-rays allowed
Blocked cat: Profile Only
UA/UPC/culture/BP: CMO approval required
