Diagnostic Algorithm

Pruritis (Itchy Skin)

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with itching, scratching, licking, chewing, or inflamed skin.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Pruritis is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically perform skin scrape, cytology, tape prep, flea comb diagnostics, Woods lamp/DTM culture, CBC/diff, chemistry, urinalysis, T4, allergy testing, biopsy, dermatology consult, or endocrine workup unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
PRURITIS / ITCHY SKIN

History

  • Onset, duration, seasonality, and progression
  • Location of itching, licking, chewing, or hair loss
  • Flea/tick prevention history
  • Diet changes, treats, environment, or contact exposures
  • Other Pets or people affected
  • Prior skin/ear infections, allergies, or treatments

Physical Exam

  • Skin redness, crusting, scaling, pustules, or wounds
  • Hair loss pattern and distribution
  • Evidence of fleas, flea dirt, mites, or parasites
  • Ear involvement or otitis signs
  • Paw, face, belly, groin, and tail-base assessment
  • Systemic illness signs or other protocol triggers

Protocol Check

  • Respiratory distress?
  • Pale mucous membranes?
  • Bleeding, toxin, or pyometra concern?
  • Not eating, vomiting, diarrhea, or PU/PD trigger?
  • Tumor suspect or mass present?
  • CMO approval needed for dermatology diagnostics?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does Another Hannah Protocol Trigger Apply?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

CBC + SHORT PROFILE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Not eating trigger is met
  • ☐ Productive vomiting for more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Dog/Cat over 1 year with diarrhea for more than 72 hrs
  • ☐ Puppy diarrhea under 1 year with no vaccines: CBC, Parvo, Diff

OTHER APPROVED TRIGGERS

  • ☐ PU/PD: CBC + Profile with Electrolytes
  • ☐ Tumor suspect: 3-view radiographs
  • ☐ Listed x-ray trigger applies

PRURITIS ALONE

  • ☐ No automatic diagnostics are approved for pruritis alone.
  • ☐ Document history, distribution, lesions, and flea/tick prevention status.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
↓
STEP 2

If Dermatology Diagnostics Are Needed

Skin scrapes, cytology, cultures, allergy testing, food trials, endocrine workup, and biopsy are above the Diagnostic Testing Protocols unless specifically approved.

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Deep or superficial skin scrape
  • ☐ Skin impression, cytology, tape prep, or micro exam
  • ☐ Woods lamp / DTM culture
  • ☐ CBC/diff, chemistry, urinalysis, T4
  • ☐ Allergy testing, elimination diet diagnostics, biopsy
  • ☐ Dermatology referral or advanced workup

SUPPORTIVE ACTIONS

  • ☐ Document lesion location and severity.
  • ☐ Review flea/tick prevention status.
  • ☐ Prevent self-trauma if indicated.
  • ☐ Follow CMO-approved plan once received.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Pruritis Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for itchy skin alone. Document distribution, severity, lesions, parasite prevention history, diet, seasonality, and prior treatments.

Pruritis + Otic Signs

Follow the Otic Disorder workflow if ear pain, odor, discharge, or head shaking is present. Ear cytology and culture still require CMO approval.

Pruritis + Mass or Tumor Suspect

Three-view radiographs are protocol-approved only if tumor suspect. FNA, cytology, biopsy, histopathology, or staging require CMO approval.

Pruritis + Systemic Trigger

If another protocol trigger applies, follow that workflow. Run only the diagnostics specifically approved by that trigger.

Recurrent or Non-Responsive Pruritis

Recurrent, severe, or non-responsive itching may need dermatology workup, but diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Suspected Allergy or Food-Related Dermatitis

Allergy testing, elimination diet trials used diagnostically, biopsy, or dermatology referral require CMO approval unless separately authorized.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Deep skin scrape, superficial skin scrape, skin impression, cytology, tape test, micro exam, flea comb diagnostic testing, Woods lamp, DTM culture, CBC/diff when no protocol trigger applies, chemistry screen, urinalysis, T4, bacterial or fungal culture, biopsy, allergy testing, elimination diet trial diagnostics, dermatology consult, endocrine workup, referral diagnostics, or other dermatologic diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Monitoring / Escalation

Escalate if itching is severe, skin is infected or painful, lesions are spreading, the Pet is self-traumatizing, ears are involved, systemic illness appears, or there is poor response to the approved plan. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Pruritis has many causes: allergy, parasites, and infection are common.
  • Indoor Pets can still be affected by parasites.
  • Secondary infections are common and can worsen itching.
  • Do not automatically run dermatology diagnostics above protocol.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Confirm pruritis and obtain complete history.
  2. Perform physical exam and document lesion distribution.
  3. Review flea/tick prevention, diet, seasonality, and prior treatments.
  4. Determine whether another protocol trigger applies.
  5. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for dermatology diagnostics above protocol.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, approvals, treatments, client education, and response.

Protocol Summary

Pruritis alone: No automatic diagnostics
Skin scrape/cytology/tape prep: CMO approval required
Woods lamp/DTM/culture: CMO approval required
Allergy testing/biopsy/endocrine workup: CMO approval required
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
