Diagnostic Algorithm

Sneezing or Nasal Discharge

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with sneezing, congestion, or nasal discharge.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Sneezing or nasal discharge is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically run CBC/diff, biochemical profile, fecal flotation, FeLV/FIV, nasal flush, culture, cytology, viral testing, fungal serology, rhinoscopy, biopsy, CT, or referral diagnostics unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
SNEEZING OR NASAL DISCHARGE

History

  • Onset, duration, and progression
  • Unilateral vs. bilateral discharge
  • Clear, mucoid, purulent, or bloody discharge
  • Indoor/outdoor status, travel, hunting, digging, or foreign body exposure
  • New Pets, boarding, daycare, or sick contacts
  • Vaccination status and prior respiratory disease

Physical Exam

  • Temperature and vital parameters
  • Respiratory effort and nasal airflow
  • Nasal symmetry and facial swelling
  • Oral/dental exam as tolerated
  • Ocular discharge or conjunctivitis
  • Thoracic auscultation and lymph node assessment

Protocol Check

  • Respiratory distress?
  • Pale mucous membranes?
  • Not eating trigger met?
  • Productive vomiting or diarrhea trigger?
  • Tumor suspect?
  • Foreign body or trauma concern?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Is This Respiratory Distress?

YES — PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ CBC is protocol-approved.
  • ☐ X-rays are protocol-approved.
  • ☐ Escalate promptly due to patient risk.
  • ☐ Additional respiratory diagnostics require CMO approval.

NO RESPIRATORY DISTRESS

  • ☐ Do not run automatic respiratory/nasal diagnostics.
  • ☐ Continue history, exam, and protocol trigger check.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request if testing is needed.
↓
STEP 2

Does Another Listed Trigger Apply?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

X-RAYS APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Tumor suspect, 3 views
  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Obvious broken bone or dislocated joint
  • ☐ Other listed x-ray trigger applies

CBC + SHORT PROFILE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and less than 1 year old
  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and over 8 years old
  • ☐ Not eating for 72 hrs and 2–8 years old
  • ☐ Productive vomiting for more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Diarrhea trigger applies

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not run automatic diagnostics for sneezing/nasal discharge alone.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Sneezing/Nasal Discharge Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for sneezing or nasal discharge alone. Document history, discharge type, duration, laterality, exposure history, and physical exam findings.

Respiratory Distress Present

CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved when respiratory distress is present. Escalate promptly and monitor patient stability.

Tumor Suspect

Three-view radiographs are protocol-approved if tumor suspect. Rhinoscopy, biopsy, CT, cytology, and referral diagnostics require CMO approval.

Foreign Body Concern

Nasal flush, rhinoscopy, sedation/anesthesia, imaging beyond listed triggers, and removal procedures require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Nasal Discharge + Systemic Signs

If another protocol trigger applies, follow that workflow. Otherwise, bloodwork, infectious disease testing, and imaging require CMO approval.

Chronic or Unilateral Discharge

Chronic or unilateral discharge may need advanced diagnostics, but those require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

CBC/diff when no protocol trigger applies, biochemical profile, fecal flotation, FeLV/FIV, nasal flush, cytology, bacterial culture/sensitivity, viral testing, fungal serology, conjunctival scraping, skull/nasal/dental radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, chest radiographs outside protocol triggers, rhinoscopy, nasal biopsy, CT, internal medicine consultation, specialty referral, or other nasal/respiratory diagnostics require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Monitoring / Escalation

Escalate if respiratory effort worsens, discharge becomes bloody, unilateral discharge persists, facial swelling develops, appetite declines, systemic signs appear, or foreign body/tumor concern increases. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Acute onset often suggests infectious, irritant, or environmental causes.
  • Unilateral discharge may indicate foreign body, polyp, neoplasia, or dental disease.
  • Bilateral discharge may suggest infectious, allergic, or systemic disease.
  • Do not automatically run baseline nasal/respiratory diagnostics.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history.
  2. Perform physical exam with nasal, oral, respiratory, and ocular observations.
  3. Determine acute vs. chronic and unilateral vs. bilateral.
  4. Screen for respiratory distress and other protocol triggers.
  5. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, approvals, treatments, and follow-up plan.

Protocol Summary

Sneezing/nasal discharge alone: No automatic diagnostics
Respiratory distress: CBC + x-rays allowed
Tumor suspect: 3-view x-rays allowed
Nasal flush/rhinoscopy/culture/viral testing/CT: CMO approval required
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
