Lameness

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets presenting with limping, pain, weakness, or mobility changes.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Lameness is a clinical sign, not a diagnosis. Under Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols, radiographs are automatically approved only when a listed x-ray trigger applies, including obvious broken bone or dislocated joint, vehicular trauma, or paresis/paralysis. CBC/diff, chemistry/internal organ screen, electrolytes, urinalysis, tick testing, joint tap, culture, ANA, myelogram, CSF analysis, EEG, nerve testing, muscle biopsy, or other diagnostics require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless another protocol trigger applies.

LEVEL 1
LAMENESS / MOBILITY CONCERN

History

  • Onset, duration, progression, and severity
  • Acute injury, fall, jump, rough play, or trauma
  • Vehicular trauma or known accident
  • Weight-bearing vs. non-weight-bearing lameness
  • Pain, swelling, wounds, fever, lethargy, or appetite change
  • Tick exposure, travel, or previous orthopedic disease

Exam

  • Gait assessment
  • Pain localization
  • Orthopedic exam as tolerated
  • Neurologic screen
  • Range of motion and joint swelling
  • Check paws, nails, pads, skin, and wounds

Protocol Check

  • Vehicular trauma?
  • Paresis or paralysis?
  • Tumor suspect?
  • CMO approval needed?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does a Listed X-Ray Trigger Apply?

X-RAYS APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Obvious broken bone
  • ☐ Dislocated joint
  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Paresis/paralysis
  • ☐ Tumor suspect, 3 views

NO X-RAY TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not take radiographs automatically.
  • ☐ Document exam findings and pain localization.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
↓
STEP 2

Are Additional Diagnostics Needed?

Additional lameness diagnostics are above the Diagnostic Testing Protocols unless another listed trigger applies. Submit the Diagnostic & Requisition request and wait for CMO approval before proceeding.

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ CBC/diff or internal organ screen without another trigger
  • ☐ Electrolytes or urinalysis
  • ☐ Tick titers / 3Dx / 4Dx testing
  • ☐ Joint tap, joint culture, or ANA
  • ☐ Myelogram, CSF analysis, EEG
  • ☐ Creatinine kinase, nerve conduction, ACH antibodies, muscle biopsy

SUPPORTIVE ACTIONS

  • ☐ Keep Pet comfortable and minimize stress.
  • ☐ Communicate pain, mobility, and neuro concerns promptly.
  • ☐ Follow CMO-approved plan once received.
  • ☐ Document response and changes.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Obvious Broken Bone or Dislocated Joint

Radiographs are protocol-approved. Stabilize and minimize manipulation. Escalate promptly for pain management and treatment planning.

Vehicular Trauma

Radiographs are protocol-approved. Assess stability, pain, respiratory status, and perfusion. Additional diagnostics require CMO approval unless another trigger applies.

Paresis or Paralysis

Radiographs are protocol-approved. Escalate immediately because neurologic compromise may require urgent intervention.

Tumor Suspect

Three-view radiographs are protocol-approved if tumor suspect. Additional oncology workup, cytology, biopsy, or staging requires CMO approval.

Lameness Without Listed Trigger

No automatic diagnostics are approved solely for lameness. Document localization, severity, exam findings, and submit request if diagnostics are needed.

Systemic Illness or Other Complaint Present

Follow the matching protocol if another trigger applies, such as pale mucous membranes, respiratory distress, not eating, vomiting, diarrhea, or tumor suspect.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Radiographs without a listed x-ray trigger, CBC/diff, internal organ screen, electrolytes, urinalysis, tick titers, 3Dx/4Dx testing, joint tap, joint culture, ANA, myelogram, CSF analysis, EEG, creatinine kinase, nerve conduction testing, ACH antibodies, muscle biopsy, advanced imaging, referral diagnostics, or other lameness workup require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Monitoring / Escalation

Reassess if pain worsens, the Pet becomes non-weight-bearing, neurologic signs develop, swelling increases, trauma is disclosed, systemic illness appears, or the Pet fails to improve. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Lameness is a sign, not a diagnosis.
  • Localize the problem before pursuing diagnostics.
  • X-rays are not automatic unless a listed trigger applies.
  • Paresis/paralysis, trauma, fracture, dislocation, and tumor suspect are key protocol triggers.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history.
  2. Perform gait assessment, physical exam, neurologic screen, and orthopedic exam as tolerated.
  3. Determine whether a listed x-ray trigger applies.
  4. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  5. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  6. Route for CMO approval when required.
  7. Support comfort measures and treatment plan as directed.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatments, and response.

Protocol Summary

Broken bone/dislocation: X-rays allowed
Vehicular trauma: X-rays allowed
Paresis/paralysis: X-rays allowed
Tumor suspect: 3-view x-rays allowed
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
