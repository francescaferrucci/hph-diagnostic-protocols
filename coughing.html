Diagnostic Algorithm

Coughing

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets presenting with cough, gagging, or suspected respiratory signs.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Coughing alone is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. If respiratory distress is present, CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved. Do not automatically run chemistry, urinalysis, blood gas, heartworm testing, fungal testing, airway wash/BAL, culture, echocardiogram, NT-proBNP, fluoroscopy, bronchoscopy, CT, or referral diagnostics unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
PATIENT WITH COUGH

History

  • Confirm coughing vs. gagging, retching, or reverse sneezing
  • Duration, frequency, and progression
  • Productive vs. non-productive cough
  • Exercise-induced, nighttime, or excitement-related cough
  • Exposure to other animals, boarding, travel, smoke, dust, or chemicals
  • Concurrent signs: fever, lethargy, appetite change, dyspnea, collapse

Physical Exam

  • Respiratory effort and rate
  • Mucous membrane color
  • Thoracic auscultation
  • Heart murmur or gallop
  • Temperature and full vital parameters
  • Stress level and ability to tolerate exam

Emergency Screen

  • Open-mouth breathing?
  • Cyanosis?
  • Collapse?
  • Severe dyspnea?
  • Shock signs?
  • Needs stabilization before diagnostics?

LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Coughing Alone

No automatic diagnostics are approved for coughing alone. Document cough character, duration, frequency, exposure history, auscultation findings, and respiratory effort.

Coughing + Respiratory Distress

CBC and x-rays are protocol-approved. Stabilize first, minimize stress, and escalate immediately. Additional diagnostics require CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Coughing + Pale Mucous Membranes

CBC is protocol-approved. Additional respiratory, cardiac, infectious, or imaging diagnostics require CMO approval unless another listed trigger applies.

Coughing + Tumor Suspect

Three-view radiographs are protocol-approved if tumor suspect. Further staging, cytology, ultrasound, CT, or referral diagnostics require CMO approval.

Coughing + Trauma

X-rays are protocol-approved for vehicular trauma. Additional diagnostics require CMO approval unless another listed protocol trigger applies.

Suspected Cardiac or Airway Disease

Echocardiogram, NT-proBNP, airway wash, bronchoscopy, fluoroscopy, or specialty diagnostics require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Chemistry, urinalysis, blood gas, heartworm testing, fungal antigen/PCR, Baermann fecal, infectious disease testing, airway wash/BAL, cytology, culture and sensitivity, fluoroscopy, endoscopy, echocardiogram, NT-proBNP, CT, bronchoscopy, specialty consultation, referral diagnostics, or other coughing/respiratory workup above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless emergently directed.

Monitoring / Escalation

Escalate immediately if the Pet develops respiratory distress, open-mouth breathing, cyanosis, collapse, worsening cough, abnormal lung sounds, fever, severe lethargy, or poor response to initial care. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Coughing is a symptom, not a diagnosis.
  • Confirm cough vs. reverse sneezing, gagging, or retching.
  • Always stabilize first if the Pet is in respiratory distress.
  • Respiratory distress allows CBC and x-rays under protocol.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Confirm the Pet is truly coughing.
  2. Obtain complete history.
  3. Assess respiratory effort and stability.
  4. Stabilize first if respiratory distress is present.
  5. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, approvals, diagnostics, treatment, and monitoring plan.

Protocol Summary

Coughing alone: No automatic diagnostics
Respiratory distress: CBC + x-rays allowed
Pale MM / bleeding / toxin / pyometra: CBC allowed
Tumor suspect: 3-view x-rays allowed
Other respiratory diagnostics: CMO approval required
