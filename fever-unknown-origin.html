Diagnostic Algorithm

Fever of Unknown Origin

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets with fever lasting more than 2–3 days with no obvious cause.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Fever of unknown origin is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically run CBC, chemistry, urinalysis, fecal testing, FeLV/FIV, heartworm testing, T4, cultures, infectious disease testing, imaging, immune testing, or specialty diagnostics unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
FEVER OF UNKNOWN ORIGIN

History

  • Duration and temperature trend
  • Appetite and water intake
  • Lethargy, pain, cough, vomiting, diarrhea, or urinary signs
  • Travel, tick exposure, wildlife exposure, boarding, or daycare
  • Medications, toxins, wounds, trauma, or recent procedures
  • Vaccination and prevention history

Physical Exam

  • Temperature and full vital parameters
  • Mucous membranes and hydration status
  • Oral exam and lymph node assessment
  • Thoracic auscultation
  • Abdominal palpation
  • Skin, wounds, joints, neurologic, and ocular exam

Protocol Check

  • Respiratory distress?
  • Pale mucous membranes?
  • Bleeding, toxin, or pyometra concern?
  • Not eating trigger met?
  • Vomiting or diarrhea trigger met?
  • Straining, trauma, tumor suspect, or other x-ray trigger?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does Another Hannah Protocol Trigger Apply?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

CBC + SHORT PROFILE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and less than 1 year old
  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and over 8 years old
  • ☐ Not eating for 72 hrs and 2–8 years old
  • ☐ Productive vomiting for more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Dog/Cat over 1 year with diarrhea for more than 72 hrs

X-RAYS APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Tumor suspect, 3 views
  • ☐ Vehicular trauma
  • ☐ Vomiting more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Non-productive vomiting or suspected foreign body
  • ☐ Straining/no urination or defecation trigger

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not run automatic diagnostics for FUO.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before proceeding.

STEP 2

Aggressively Document Localizing Clues

Revisit history and physical exam findings often. FUO workups should be guided by localizing signs, trends, and CMO-approved diagnostics rather than broad automatic testing.

LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Fever With No Other Protocol Trigger

No automatic diagnostics are approved solely for fever. Document exam findings, temperature trend, exposure history, and submit Diagnostic & Requisition request if diagnostics are needed.

Fever + Not Eating

If the Pet meets the age and duration criteria for not eating, CBC and Short Profile are protocol-approved. Otherwise, additional diagnostics require CMO approval.

Fever + Respiratory Distress

CBC is protocol-approved, and x-rays are protocol-approved when respiratory distress is present. Escalate promptly due to patient risk.

Fever + Pale Mucous Membranes

CBC is protocol-approved. Reticulocyte count, smear review, chemistry, infectious disease testing, or other anemia workup requires CMO approval unless another trigger applies.

Fever + Productive Vomiting or Diarrhea Trigger

Follow the vomiting or diarrhea workflow when those triggers apply. Run only the specific protocol-approved diagnostics for that presentation.

Fever + Localizing X-Ray Trigger

X-rays are protocol-approved only if a listed x-ray trigger applies, such as respiratory distress, tumor suspect, trauma, vomiting more than 48 hours, non-productive vomiting, suspected foreign body, straining to urinate/defecate, or no urination/defecation for 48 hours.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

CBC/differential when no protocol trigger applies, full chemistry/internal organ screen, urinalysis, fecal flotation, heartworm test, FeLV/FIV, T4, blood culture, urine culture, fecal culture, thoracic or abdominal radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, abdominal ultrasound, tick-borne disease testing, fungal serology, FIP testing, Coombs, rheumatoid factor, ANA, lymph node aspirate, bone marrow aspirate/biopsy, joint tap, CSF tap, echocardiography, antibiotic trial, glucocorticoid trial, referral diagnostics, or other FUO workup require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Reassessment / Escalation

If fever persists, worsens, or localizing signs develop, reassess the Pet, update the history, document new findings, and route the case for CMO approval before pursuing diagnostics above the Diagnostic Testing Protocols.

Critter Clues

  • FUO is a diagnosis of exclusion.
  • Reevaluate frequently and monitor trends.
  • Localizing signs should guide the workup.
  • Do not automatically run broad FUO testing.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history and perform comprehensive physical exam.
  2. Confirm fever duration and temperature trend.
  3. Look for localizing signs or another protocol-approved trigger.
  4. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  5. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for FUO diagnostics above protocol.
  6. Route for CMO approval when required.
  7. Recheck frequently and update history as needed.
  8. Document findings, approvals, treatments, and outcomes.

Protocol Summary

FUO alone: No automatic diagnostics
Respiratory distress / pale MM / bleeding / toxin / pyometra: CBC
Approved not-eating, vomiting, or diarrhea trigger: CBC + Short Profile
Listed x-ray trigger: X-rays allowed
Other FUO diagnostics: CMO approval required
