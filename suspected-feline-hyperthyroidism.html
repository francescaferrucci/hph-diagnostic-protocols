Diagnostic Algorithm

Suspected Feline Hyperthyroidism

Protocol-aligned workflow for cats with clinical signs suggestive of hyperthyroidism.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Suspected feline hyperthyroidism is not listed as an automatic diagnostic testing trigger in the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols. Do not automatically run Total T4, Free T4 by equilibrium dialysis, T3 suppression testing, CBC, chemistry profile, electrolytes, urinalysis, or follow-up thyroid testing unless another listed protocol trigger applies. Diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

LEVEL 1
SUSPECTED FELINE HYPERTHYROIDISM

History

  • Weight loss with normal or increased appetite
  • Vomiting or diarrhea
  • Increased thirst or urination
  • Hyperactivity, restlessness, vocalization, or behavior change
  • Poor coat quality or unkempt appearance
  • Current medications, prior illness, or prior thyroid testing

Physical Exam

  • Body weight and body condition trend
  • Heart rate and rhythm
  • Respiratory rate and effort
  • Hydration status
  • Coat quality and muscle condition
  • Palpation for thyroid slip or cervical mass if appropriate

Protocol Check

  • Does the cat meet age-based new patient testing?
  • Not eating trigger met?
  • Vomiting or diarrhea trigger met?
  • PU/PD trigger present?
  • Respiratory distress or pale mucous membranes?
  • CMO approval needed for thyroid testing?

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does Another Hannah Protocol Trigger Apply?

NEW HEALTHY CAT TESTING MAY APPLY

  • ☐ Healthy cat 9+ years: CBC, V/A, TD Profile
  • ☐ Healthy cat 12–14 years: CBC, V/A, TD Profile
  • ☐ Healthy cat 15–17 years: CBC, V/A, TD Profile
  • ☐ Healthy cat 18+ years: CBC, V/A, TD Profile

CBC + SHORT PROFILE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and over 8 years old
  • ☐ Not eating for 72 hrs and 2–8 years old
  • ☐ Productive vomiting for more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Dog/Cat over 1 year with diarrhea for more than 72 hrs

OTHER PROTOCOL TRIGGERS

  • ☐ PU/PD: CBC + Profile with Electrolytes
  • ☐ Respiratory distress: CBC; x-rays allowed
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes: CBC
  • ☐ Bleeding, toxin, or pyometra concern: CBC

NO PROTOCOL TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not run automatic thyroid or baseline diagnostics.
  • ☐ Document clinical signs and exam findings.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
↓
STEP 2

If Thyroid Testing Is Requested

Total T4, Free T4 by equilibrium dialysis, T3 suppression testing, repeat T4 testing, and thyroid monitoring are above the Diagnostic Testing Protocols unless specifically approved. Submit the request and wait for CMO approval before proceeding.

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Total T4 when not part of an approved TD Profile
  • ☐ Free T4 by equilibrium dialysis
  • ☐ T3 suppression test
  • ☐ Repeat or follow-up T4 testing
  • ☐ CBC/chemistry/electrolytes/UA when no protocol trigger applies
  • ☐ Referral or advanced endocrine diagnostics

DOCUMENTATION

  • ☐ Record clinical signs supporting suspicion.
  • ☐ Note evidence of nonthyroidal illness.
  • ☐ Document medications that may affect thyroid results.
  • ☐ Follow CMO-approved plan once received.
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Hyperthyroid Signs Alone

No automatic thyroid diagnostics are approved for hyperthyroid signs alone. Document clinical signs and submit Diagnostic & Requisition request if testing is needed.

Eligible New Healthy Senior Cat

If the cat is being evaluated as a new healthy senior cat under the membership protocol, the applicable age-based testing may include CBC, V/A, and TD Profile.

Concurrent PU/PD

PU/PD is protocol-approved for CBC and Profile with Electrolytes. Thyroid-specific diagnostics still require CMO approval unless included in an approved profile or specifically authorized.

Concurrent Not-Eating Trigger

If the cat meets the age and duration criteria for not eating, CBC and Short Profile are protocol-approved. Thyroid testing still requires CMO approval unless otherwise authorized.

Concurrent Vomiting or Diarrhea Trigger

Follow the vomiting or diarrhea workflow when those triggers apply. Run only the specific protocol-approved diagnostics for that presentation.

Possible Nonthyroidal Illness

NTI, medications, and other illness can affect T4 results. Document concurrent illness and route diagnostics through the appropriate workflow or Diagnostic & Requisition request.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Total T4 when not included in an approved TD Profile, Free T4 by equilibrium dialysis, T3 suppression testing, repeat or follow-up T4, CBC when no protocol trigger applies, chemistry profile, electrolytes, urinalysis, blood pressure, advanced endocrine testing, referral diagnostics, thyroid treatment monitoring, or other diagnostics above protocol require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Monitoring / Escalation

Reassess if clinical signs worsen, appetite changes, vomiting/diarrhea develops, PU/PD becomes significant, weight loss progresses, or medication changes occur. Document response, client communication, recheck timing, and any CMO-approved monitoring plan.

Critter Clues

  • Feline hyperthyroidism is common in cats over 7 years of age.
  • Common signs include weight loss with increased appetite, PU/PD, vomiting, hyperactivity, and poor coat quality.
  • NTI, illness, and medications can affect Total T4 results.
  • Thyroid testing is not automatic unless it falls under an approved protocol.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history and document clinical signs.
  2. Perform physical exam and assess for concurrent illness.
  3. Determine whether another protocol trigger applies.
  4. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  5. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for thyroid diagnostics above protocol.
  6. Route for CMO approval when required.
  7. Support sample collection and monitoring only after approval.
  8. Document findings, approvals, client conversations, treatment response, and rechecks.

Protocol Summary

Hyperthyroid signs alone: No automatic diagnostics
Eligible senior new healthy cat: CBC + V/A + TD Profile
PU/PD: CBC + Profile with Electrolytes
Total T4 / Free T4 / T3 suppression: CMO approval required unless included in approved protocol
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
