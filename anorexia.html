Diagnostic Algorithm

Anorexia

Protocol-aligned workflow for Pets who are not eating or have reduced appetite.

Protocol-aligned
May 2026

Testing Guardrail

Under the Hannah Diagnostic Testing Protocols, not eating only authorizes CBC and Short Profile when specific age and duration criteria are met: less than 1 year old and not eating for 48 hours, over 8 years old and not eating for 48 hours, or 2–8 years old and not eating for 72 hours. Fecal testing, urinalysis, chemistry panels beyond protocol, electrolytes, 3Dx, FeLV/FIV, T4/Free T4, radiographs, SNAP cPL, ultrasound, endoscopy, biopsy, feeding tube placement, or other diagnostics require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval unless another listed protocol trigger applies.

LEVEL 1
ANOREXIA / NOT EATING

History

  • Duration of not eating or reduced appetite
  • Age of Pet
  • Diet change, treats, garbage, toxins, or medications
  • Vomiting, diarrhea, coughing, urinary signs, or pain
  • Stress, environment change, travel, or new Pets
  • Prior illness or chronic disease

Physical Exam

  • Hydration status
  • Temperature and vital parameters
  • Mucous membrane color
  • Oral and dental exam
  • Abdominal palpation
  • Neurologic and pain assessment

Protocol Check

  • Less than 1 year old and not eating 48 hours?
  • Over 8 years old and not eating 48 hours?
  • 2–8 years old and not eating 72 hours?
  • Vomiting or diarrhea trigger?
  • Respiratory distress, pale MM, bleeding, toxin, or pyometra?
  • Listed x-ray trigger present?
↓

Determine Protocol-Approved Testing

STEP 1

Does the Pet Meet a Not-Eating Testing Trigger?

CBC + SHORT PROFILE APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and less than 1 year old
  • ☐ Not eating for 48 hrs and over 8 years old
  • ☐ Not eating for 72 hrs and 2–8 years old
  • ☐ Document age, duration, and clinical signs

NO NOT-EATING TRIGGER

  • ☐ Do not run automatic anorexia diagnostics.
  • ☐ Document history, appetite timeline, and exam findings.
  • ☐ Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request if diagnostics are needed.
  • ☐ CMO approval required before diagnostics above protocol.
↓
STEP 2

Does Another Protocol Trigger Apply?

CBC APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Respiratory distress
  • ☐ Pale mucous membranes
  • ☐ Bleeding concern
  • ☐ Toxin concern
  • ☐ Pyometra concern

OTHER CBC + SHORT PROFILE TRIGGERS

  • ☐ Productive vomiting for more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Dog/Cat over 1 year with diarrhea for more than 72 hrs
  • ☐ Puppy diarrhea under 1 year with no vaccines: CBC, Parvo, Diff

X-RAYS APPROVED IF

  • ☐ Vomiting more than 48 hrs
  • ☐ Non-productive vomiting
  • ☐ Suspected foreign body
  • ☐ Straining/no urination or defecation trigger
  • ☐ Trauma, tumor suspect, or respiratory distress

CMO APPROVAL REQUIRED

  • ☐ Fecal testing, UA, 3Dx, FeLV/FIV, T4/Free T4
  • ☐ SNAP cPL, ultrasound, endoscopy, biopsy
  • ☐ Feeding tube or advanced nutritional diagnostics
  • ☐ Any diagnostics above listed protocol triggers
LEVEL 2

Protocol-Approved Pathways

Less Than 1 Year Old + Not Eating 48 Hours

Protocol-approved diagnostics: CBC and Short Profile. Document appetite timeline, hydration, physical exam findings, and any vomiting, diarrhea, or vaccine concerns.

Over 8 Years Old + Not Eating 48 Hours

Protocol-approved diagnostics: CBC and Short Profile. Additional testing beyond this requires CMO approval unless another protocol trigger applies.

2–8 Years Old + Not Eating 72 Hours

Protocol-approved diagnostics: CBC and Short Profile. Document duration clearly before running diagnostics.

Anorexia Less Than Protocol Duration

No automatic diagnostics are approved for anorexia alone if age and duration criteria are not met. Monitor, support care as directed, and submit request if testing is needed.

Anorexia + Vomiting or Diarrhea

Follow the Acute Vomiting or Diarrhea workflow if those triggers apply. Run only the specific protocol-approved diagnostics for that presentation.

Concern for Foreign Body or Obstruction

X-rays are protocol-approved when suspected foreign body, vomiting more than 48 hours, or non-productive vomiting is present. Other diagnostics require CMO approval.

LEVEL 3

CMO Approval Required Diagnostics

Fecal flotation, saline wet mount, fecal cytology, urinalysis, CBC/diff or chemistry when no protocol trigger applies, internal organ screen, electrolytes, 3Dx, FeLV/FIV, T4, Free T4, abdominal or chest radiographs outside listed x-ray triggers, barium series, SNAP cPL, abdominal ultrasound, endoscopy with biopsy, exploratory surgery, feeding tube placement, referral diagnostics, or any additional anorexia workup require Diagnostic & Requisition submission and CMO approval.

Monitoring / Escalation

Escalate if the Pet becomes weak, dehydrated, painful, febrile, vomiting, diarrheic, jaundiced, dyspneic, unable to keep food down, or continues not eating despite supportive care. Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

Critter Clues

  • Anorexia is a clinical sign, not a diagnosis.
  • Age and duration determine whether CBC + Short Profile are protocol-approved.
  • Document appetite timeline clearly.
  • Do not automatically run broad anorexia diagnostics.
  • Diagnostics above protocol require CMO approval.

PetNurse Workflow

  1. Obtain detailed history and appetite timeline.
  2. Perform complete physical exam with oral and neurologic screen.
  3. Confirm age and duration of not eating.
  4. Determine whether a protocol trigger applies.
  5. Run only protocol-approved diagnostics.
  6. Submit Diagnostic & Requisition request for diagnostics above protocol.
  7. Route for CMO approval when required.
  8. Document findings, approvals, treatments, nutrition plan, and response.

Protocol Summary

<1 year + not eating 48 hrs: CBC + Short Profile
>8 years + not eating 48 hrs: CBC + Short Profile
2–8 years + not eating 72 hrs: CBC + Short Profile
Other diagnostics: CMO approval required
